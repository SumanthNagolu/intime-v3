## Part 10: Risk Management & Mitigation

### Business Risks

**Risk: Students don't get placed**
- **Likelihood:** Medium (30% in bad market)
- **Impact:** High (reputation damage, refunds, churn)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Job placement conditional on certification level (manage expectations)
  - Can't promise job, but provide best support
  - Placement rate transparency (publish real numbers)
  - Refund policy: Only if we're at fault (not market conditions)
  - Partner with recruiting pod (internal advantage)

**Risk: Recruiting pods miss 2 placements/sprint target**
- **Likelihood:** Medium (market-dependent)
- **Impact:** High (revenue miss, employee churn, client dissatisfaction)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Performance-based compensation (aligned incentives)
  - Process documentation (reduce person-dependency)
  - Peer review + management review (fairness)
  - Warning system: 7 misses â†’ verbal, 10 â†’ written, 13 â†’ notice
  - Continuous training and support

**Risk: AI costs spike unexpectedly**
- **Likelihood:** Low (but possible)
- **Impact:** Medium (margin compression)
- **Mitigation:**
  - High-ticket industry (can justify costs)
  - Monitor usage closely (alerts)
  - Optimize regularly (model selection, batching)
  - Pass some cost to customers if needed

**Risk: Key employees leave with clients/knowledge**
- **Likelihood:** Medium (always possible)
- **Impact:** High (revenue loss, operational disruption)
- **Mitigation:**
  - 100% process-oriented (everything documented)
  - AI captures institutional knowledge
  - Client relationships spread across team
  - Non-compete agreements (where legal)
  - Culture of growth and equity participation

**Risk: Competitor copies the model**
- **Likelihood:** High (inevitable)
- **Impact:** Medium (market share pressure)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Speed (first-mover advantage)
  - Proprietary AI (learns from our data)
  - Network effects (more users = better product)
  - Brand and trust (established reputation)
  - Constant innovation (always ahead)

### Technical Risks

**Risk: Database breach / data leak**
- **Mitigation:**
  - RLS on all tables (database-level security)
  - Regular security audits
  - Penetration testing (annual)
  - Compliance: SOC 2 (Year 2), GDPR
  - Encryption at rest and in transit
  - Minimal data retention (delete old data)

**Risk: AI hallucinates and gives bad advice**
- **Mitigation:**
  - Training datasets (supervised learning)
  - Human review for critical decisions
  - Disclaimer: "AI is a tool, not a replacement"
  - Feedback loop (users report bad advice)
  - Continuous model improvement

**Risk: Supabase/Vercel downtime**
- **Mitigation:**
  - SLA: 99.9% uptime guarantee from vendors
  - Monitoring: Real-time alerts (Sentry, Better Uptime)
  - Backups: Daily database backups (Supabase)
  - Failover: Plan for migration if needed (unlikely)
  - Communication: Status page for users

**Risk: Integration breaks between pods**
- **Mitigation:**
  - Event-driven architecture (loose coupling)
  - Automated testing (E2E tests)
  - Staging environment (test before production)
  - Rollback plan (version control)

### Regulatory & Compliance Risks

**Training Academy:**
- Not an accredited institution (no claims of accreditation)
- Consumer protection: Full disclosure, no false promises
- Data privacy: GDPR (if international), CCPA (California)
- Refund policy: Clearly stated (no refunds except fault)

**Recruiting:**
- Employment law: Anti-discrimination compliance
- Background checks: Required for some roles
- Wage and hour: Consultant classification (1099 vs. W2)
- No special licensing required (staffing industry)

**Cross-Border:**
- Immigration law: Partner with licensed lawyers (not in-house)
- Tax: Partner with CAs for international payments
- Compliance: Industry-standard (not aggressive)

**General Approach:**
- "Try to stay away as much as we can" (avoid over-regulation)
- Partner with experts (lawyers, CAs) rather than in-house
- Transparency and honesty (manage expectations)
- Industry-standard compliance (not cutting-edge risky)

## Part 13: Founder Principles & Decision Framework

### Codified Principles

**Quality Standard: "Best, only the best, nothing but the best"**

**Operational Meaning:**

- **For Hiring:**
  - Always run internships with training-to-hire model
  - 3-month trial period with structured evaluation
  - Retain and expand with good candidates
  - Let go of others and keep searching
  - Goal: 50%+ hiring from our own training by Year 2
  - Rest: Pure will and passion (good communication + story-telling skills + AI interaction ability)

- **For Student Acceptance:**
  - Dedication, accountability, basic understanding
  - "Time/effort in = quality/skill out" mindset
  - No shortcuts, no entitlement

- **For Client Selection:**
  - Values alignment (mutual respect)
  - Profitability (fair compensation)
  - Long-term partnership potential

- **For Feature Prioritization:**
  - All features equal importance (holistic organism)
  - But quality over quantity
  - Okay for financial loss, NEVER for reputational loss

### Decision-Making Patterns

**Example 1: Should we offer refunds for students who don't get placed?**
- **Thought Process:**
  - Job market is beyond our control
  - We provide best training, not guaranteed employment
  - But if we're at fault (poor training), we should take responsibility
- **Decision:**
  - No refunds as promise (manage expectations)
  - But refund if we fail (poor content, broken promises)
  - Sense of responsibility, not entitlement
- **Rationale:**
  - Protect reputation (not money)
  - Honesty and transparency

**Example 2: Should we hire experienced recruiters or train fresh grads?**
- **Thought Process:**
  - With AI, good communication + will + passion > experience
  - Story-telling skills to interact with AI matter more
  - Can teach recruiting, can't teach dedication
- **Decision:**
  - Hire for will and passion
  - Train via 3-month internship model
  - Retain top performers, let go of others
- **Rationale:**
  - Scalable (not dependent on scarce experienced talent)
  - Culture of growth and learning
  - AI levels the playing field

**Example 3: Build vs. Buy (Tools)?**
- **Thought Process:**
  - External tools: $35K/year, data silos, no learning
  - Build our own: $9K/year, full integration, competitive advantage
  - Time investment: 3-4 months
- **Decision:**
  - Build our own (despite longer initial timeline)
- **Rationale:**
  - Long-term thinking (5-year vision)
  - Data ownership = future optionality (B2B SaaS)
  - Living organism requires integration

**"It's not WHAT we do, it's HOW we do it"**

- Document everything (institutional knowledge)
- Evolve constantly

**"Okay for financial loss, NEVER for reputational loss"**
