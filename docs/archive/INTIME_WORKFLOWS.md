## Roles

### 1. Business Development Manager
**Mission:** Bring in new client accounts

### 2. Account Manager
**Mission:** Own client relationships, translate needs to team, ensure delivery

### 3. Back Office Recruiter (Pool Builder)
**Mission:** Build and maintain a qualified, ready-to-submit candidate pool

### 4. Front Office Recruiter (Sales Recruiter)
**Mission:** Fill jobs fast by working the pre-qualified pool

### 5. Bench Sales Rep
**Mission:** Proactively sell available consultants to clients

### 6. Direct Hire Sales Rep
**Mission:** Run campaigns to place candidates in full-time positions

### 7. Training Coordinator
**Mission:** Upskill candidates to make them more marketable

### 8. Operations Coordinator
**Mission:** Handle timesheets, payroll, compliance, onboarding

### 9. Team Manager
**Mission:** Ensure team hits targets, coach, remove blockers

### 10. Executive
**Mission:** Run the business, hit revenue/margin targets

### 11. System Admin
**Mission:** Keep the system running, secure, configured correctly

## BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT WORKFLOWS

### BD1: Identifying Target Accounts

**What happens today:**
- Research companies that might need staffing
- Build list manually
- No systematic approach
- Miss opportunities

**What should happen:**
- Define ideal client profile (industry, size, hiring patterns)
- System suggests target accounts based on criteria
- See which accounts have been approached before
- Track all target accounts in one place

**Success criteria:**
- Systematic approach to targeting
- No duplicate outreach
- Clear list of high-potential targets
- Progress tracked

---

### BD2: Outreach and Prospecting

**What happens today:**
- Cold call and email
- Track in spreadsheet
- Forget to follow up
- No insight into what works

**What should happen:**
- Structured outreach sequences
- Track all touches automatically
- Reminders for follow-ups
- See what messaging works

**Success criteria:**
- Consistent outreach cadence
- Nothing falls through cracks
- Learn from what works
- Higher response rates

---

### BD3: Qualifying New Opportunities

**What happens today:**
- Prospect shows interest
- Have discovery call
- Try to understand their needs
- Notes scattered

**What should happen:**
- Structured qualification process
- Capture key info (volume, skills needed, timeline, budget)
- Score opportunity
- Clear next steps

**Success criteria:**
- Qualified opportunities move forward
- Unqualified don't waste time
- Good notes for handoff
- Clear picture of opportunity

---

### BD4: Handing Off to Account Management

**What happens today:**
- Close the deal
- Tell account manager about new client
- Hope info gets transferred
- Sometimes things get lost

**What should happen:**
- Smooth handoff process
- All discovery info transferred
- Account manager has full context
- Client feels continuity

**Success criteria:**
- No information lost in handoff
- Client doesn't repeat themselves
- Account manager can hit ground running
- Relationship starts strong

---

### BD5: Tracking Pipeline and Forecasting

**What happens today:**
- Track deals in spreadsheet
- Guess at close probability
- Forecast is unreliable
- Don't know where to focus

**What should happen:**
- All deals in pipeline view
- Clear stages and probabilities
- Forecast based on real data
- Know which deals need attention

**Success criteria:**
- Accurate pipeline visibility
- Reliable forecast
- Focus on high-impact deals
- Hit targets
