# Regional Director - Role Overview

**Role ID:** `regional_director`
**Department:** Executive Leadership
**Reports To:** CEO / COO
**FLSA Status:** Exempt
**Location:** Regional HQ (Hybrid/Remote)

---

## Executive Summary

The Regional Director is a senior executive responsible for all operations, P&L, and strategic growth within an assigned geographic region. This role oversees multiple country operations, manages cross-border teams, ensures regulatory compliance across jurisdictions, and drives revenue growth while maintaining operational excellence.

**Scope of Authority:**
- Full P&L responsibility for region (typically $20M-$100M annual revenue)
- Oversight of 3-8 country operations
- Management of 50-300 employees across countries
- Budget authority up to regional plan
- Hiring/termination authority within region
- Contract approval authority per delegation matrix

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Regional P&L Management (35% of time)

**Revenue Responsibility:**
- Own regional revenue targets and growth objectives
- Monitor monthly/quarterly performance vs. plan
- Manage pricing strategies across countries
- Approve discounts and special pricing arrangements
- Drive upsell and cross-sell initiatives

**Cost Management:**
- Control regional operating expenses
- Manage headcount within budget
- Optimize office space and infrastructure costs
- Review and approve vendor contracts
- Monitor transfer pricing between entities

**Margin Optimization:**
- Maintain gross margin targets (28-35% by service line)
- Balance utilization vs. bench costs
- Manage client mix (direct vs. subcontractor)
- Control recruiting costs and time-to-fill
- Optimize pay/bill spreads by market

**Financial Reporting:**
- Monthly P&L review with CEO/CFO
- Quarterly business reviews
- Annual budget planning and forecasting
- Variance analysis and corrective actions
- Cash flow management and collections

### 2. Multi-Country Operations (25% of time)

**Country Leadership:**
- Manage country managers/heads
- Set country-specific targets aligned to regional goals
- Coordinate resource allocation across countries
- Balance local autonomy with regional consistency
- Resolve cross-country conflicts and disputes

**Operational Excellence:**
- Standardize processes across countries
- Implement regional technology platforms
- Drive automation and efficiency initiatives
- Ensure consistent client experience
- Share best practices across teams

**Infrastructure Management:**
- Office locations and expansions
- Technology infrastructure and tools
- Vendor relationships and contracts
- Shared services optimization
- Disaster recovery and business continuity

**Talent Management:**
- Hire and develop country leaders
- Succession planning for key roles
- Cross-country talent mobility
- Regional training and development programs
- Performance management and compensation

### 3. Compliance & Risk Management (15% of time)

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Ensure adherence to labor laws in each country
- Maintain employment compliance (visas, work permits)
- Tax compliance and transfer pricing
- Data privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Industry-specific regulations (finance, healthcare)

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Identify and assess regional risks
- Implement controls and mitigation strategies
- Monitor risk indicators and trends
- Manage insurance and liability coverage
- Crisis management and response

**Audit & Controls:**
- Internal audit compliance
- SOX controls (if applicable)
- External audit coordination
- Policy enforcement
- Ethics and compliance training

**Legal Affairs:**
- Contract review and approval
- Dispute resolution and litigation management
- Intellectual property protection
- Employment law matters
- Corporate governance

### 4. Strategic Growth & Market Expansion (15% of time)

**Market Development:**
- Identify new market opportunities
- Expansion into new countries/cities
- Service line expansion strategy
- Competitive analysis and positioning
- Partnership and alliance development

**Client Development:**
- Major account relationship management
- Regional client conferences and events
- RFP response and pricing strategy
- Client satisfaction and retention
- Cross-country client coordination

**Mergers & Acquisitions:**
- Identify acquisition targets
- Due diligence and valuation
- Integration planning and execution

**Innovation:**
- New service offerings
- Technology adoption and innovation
- Process improvement initiatives

### 5. Leadership & Culture (10% of time)

**Team Leadership:**
- Set vision and strategic direction
- Communicate regional priorities
- Build high-performing teams

**Culture & Engagement:**

## Career Path & Progression

### Path to Regional Director

**Typical Journey (12-15 years):**
1. Recruiter (2-3 years)
2. Senior Recruiter / Team Lead (2-3 years)
3. Pod Manager (3-4 years)
4. Country Manager / Director (4-5 years)
5. Regional Director

**Alternative Paths:**
- Sales leadership → Regional VP Sales → Regional Director
- Operations leadership → COO track → Regional Director
- Strategic accounts → Enterprise Sales Director → Regional Director
- External hire: VP-level from competitors

### Development Requirements

**Pre-Promotion Readiness:**
- Managed country P&L >$10M successfully for 2+ years
- Led team of 30+ employees
- Demonstrated multi-country experience
- MBA or equivalent business acumen
- Executive coaching/leadership program completion

**Onboarding for New Regional Directors (90 days):**
- Week 1-2: Meet all country managers, tour offices
- Week 3-4: Deep dive into regional P&L and operations
- Week 5-6: Client visits in each major market
- Week 7-8: Strategic planning with CEO/CFO
- Month 3: Present 90-day assessment and plan

### Progression from Regional Director

**Next Level Opportunities:**
- **Chief Operating Officer (COO):** Oversee all regions
- **Chief Revenue Officer (CRO):** Global sales and delivery
- **CEO:** Ultimate leadership role
- **Board Advisor:** Strategic counsel to leadership
- **Entrepreneur:** Start own staffing firm or related venture

**Typical Timeline:**
- 3-5 years as Regional Director before next move
- Successful track record of growth and profitability
- Demonstrated strategic thinking and innovation
- Strong leadership bench developed

## Success Profile

### First 90 Days Priorities

**Month 1 - Learn:**
- Meet every country manager and key stakeholders
- Review regional P&L, pipeline, and forecasts
- Understand current challenges and opportunities
- Visit at least one office per country
- Meet top 10 clients in region

**Month 2 - Assess:**
- Complete SWOT analysis for region
- Identify quick wins and critical issues
- Assess team capabilities and gaps
- Review processes and systems
- Benchmark against other regions

**Month 3 - Act:**
- Present strategic plan to CEO/Board
- Implement 2-3 quick wins
- Address any critical performance issues
- Set clear goals and KPIs for team
- Establish communication rhythms

### Critical Success Factors

**Year 1:**
- Achieve revenue and margin targets
- Build trust with country managers and teams
- Establish clear vision and strategy
- Address any compliance or risk issues
- Demonstrate thought leadership

**Year 2-3:**
- Drive significant revenue growth (>20% YoY)
- Improve operational efficiency and margins
- Expand into new markets or service lines
- Develop strong leadership pipeline
- Become recognized industry leader in region

**Long-Term (3+ years):**
- Sustained profitable growth
- Market leadership position in region
- Best-in-class operational metrics
- Strong succession bench
- Innovation and competitive differentiation

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Micromanaging country operations:** Trust local leaders while providing guidance
2. **Ignoring cultural differences:** Adapt management style to local norms
3. **US-centric thinking (if applicable):** Each market has unique dynamics
4. **Neglecting compliance:** Regulatory violations can be catastrophic
5. **Short-term focus:** Balance quarterly targets with long-term strategy
6. **Poor communication:** Over-communicate across timezones and cultures
7. **Failing to develop talent:** Succession planning is critical
8. **Ignoring employee engagement:** Remote teams need extra attention
9. **Underestimating complexity:** Multi-country operations have hidden challenges
10. **Not building peer relationships:** Collaborate with other regional directors

**Regional Leadership Team:**
- Regional Director (Chair)

- Regional Finance Director

- Regional HR Director

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Weekly sync (1 hour, rotating timezone)

### Decision-Making Framework

**Regional Director Authority:**
- Day-to-day operations
- Resource allocation within budget
- Hiring/termination (with HR involvement)

- Operational policy changes

**Requires CEO/CFO Approval:**
- Over-budget spending

- New country expansion
- M&A activities
- Significant policy changes

- Major capital investments (>$500K)
- Large acquisitions
- New business line launches
