## Role Summary

### Position Overview

The Account Manager (AM) is a strategic client relationship owner responsible for managing the post-sale lifecycle of enterprise staffing clients. Unlike recruiters who source and place candidates, Account Managers focus exclusively on client retention, satisfaction, expansion, and strategic partnership development.

**Primary Mission:** Maximize client lifetime value through exceptional relationship management, proactive issue resolution, and strategic growth initiatives.

### Strategic Distinction

**Account Manager Focus:**
- Strategic client relationship ownership
- Client satisfaction and health monitoring
- Contract renewals and expansions
- Executive-level stakeholder management
- Revenue retention and growth
- Cross-functional coordination

**NOT Responsible For:**
- Candidate sourcing or screening
- Interview scheduling or coordination
- Submission creation
- Direct recruiting activities
- Job posting management

### Organizational Position

```
Chief Revenue Officer / VP of Sales
    ├── Sales Director
    │   ├── Account Managers (Strategic Clients)
    │   └── Account Managers (Mid-Market Clients)
    │
    └── Recruiting Director
        ├── Recruiting Managers
        └── Recruiters (work WITH AMs, report to Recruiting)
```

### Typical Reporting Structure

**Direct Reports:** None (individual contributor role)
**Collaborates With:**
- Recruiting Managers (coordinate delivery)
- Recruiters (provide client context)
- Sales Team (handoff process)
- Operations (process improvement)
- Finance (billing, collections)
- Legal (contract negotiations)

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Client Relationship Management (40% of time)

**Strategic Partnership Development**

- Own end-to-end client relationship post-sale
- Serve as single point of contact for client escalations
- Build relationships with key stakeholders across client organization
- Map stakeholder influence and decision-making hierarchy
- Schedule regular touchpoints (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- Anticipate client needs and proactively address concerns

**Health Monitoring & Risk Management**

- Track client health scores (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Identify early warning signs of churn risk
- Monitor hiring velocity and pipeline trends
- Assess satisfaction through NPS and informal feedback
- Create risk mitigation plans for at-risk accounts
- Document all client interactions in CRM

**Executive Engagement**

- Facilitate executive sponsor relationships
- Coordinate C-level introductions and meetings
- Present strategic value propositions
- Align staffing strategy with client business goals
- Participate in client strategic planning sessions

### 2. Escalation Management (25% of time)

**Issue Resolution**

- Triage incoming escalations by severity and impact
- Own escalation resolution process end-to-end
- Coordinate cross-functional response teams
- Communicate status updates to client and internal teams
- Track resolution time and effectiveness
- Conduct post-mortem analysis

**Quality Assurance**

- Review submission quality before client delivery
- Audit interview feedback and candidate experience
- Monitor time-to-fill and SLA compliance
- Ensure recruiter adherence to client preferences
- Identify systemic issues vs one-off problems

**Root Cause Analysis**

- Investigate recurring issues or patterns
- Collaborate with operations on process improvements
- Implement preventive measures
- Share lessons learned across organization

### 3. Revenue Growth & Expansion (20% of time)

**Account Expansion**

- Identify upsell and cross-sell opportunities
- Map whitespace (untapped departments/locations)
- Introduce new service lines to existing clients
- Facilitate new hiring manager relationships
- Develop account growth plans
- Track expansion pipeline in CRM

**Contract Management**

- Lead renewal negotiations 90-180 days before expiration
- Negotiate rate increases tied to value delivery
- Expand MSA scope (new locations, divisions, job families)
- Structure volume commitments and discounts
- Coordinate with legal on contract amendments

**Pricing Strategy**

- Analyze margin by job type and client segment
- Recommend rate optimizations based on market data
- Justify rate increases with ROI analysis
- Structure creative pricing models (VMS, RPO, hybrid)

### 4. Strategic Planning & QBRs (10% of time)

**Quarterly Business Reviews**

- Prepare comprehensive QBR presentations
- Present performance metrics and trends
- Showcase success stories and case studies
- Identify areas for improvement
- Align on upcoming quarter priorities
- Document action items and follow-up

**Strategic Roadmapping**

- Develop annual account plans
- Set quarterly goals aligned with client objectives
- Forecast hiring needs based on client growth plans
- Plan resource allocation (recruiter capacity)
- Identify technology or process enhancements needed

### 5. Cross-Functional Coordination (5% of time)

**Recruiter Enablement**

- Brief recruiters on client culture and preferences
- Share competitive intelligence and market insights
- Provide feedback on submission quality
- Recognize outstanding recruiter performance
- Escalate recruiter performance issues to management

**Internal Advocacy**

- Represent client voice in internal strategy discussions
- Advocate for client-specific process exceptions
- Recommend tool or system improvements
- Share client feedback with product/operations teams

## Account Manager vs Recruiter

### Clear Role Boundaries

| Responsibility | Account Manager | Recruiter |
|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Client Relationship** | Strategic owner | Tactical execution |
| **Candidate Sourcing** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| **Submission Creation** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| **Interview Scheduling** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| **Escalation Handling** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No (escalates to AM) |
| **Contract Negotiation** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **QBR Presentation** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No (may support) |
| **Client Health Scoring** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Revenue Forecasting** | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| **Job Posting** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| **Candidate Screening** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| **Rate Negotiation** | ✅ Yes (client-side) | ✅ Yes (candidate-side) |

### Collaboration Model

**Recruiter → AM Escalation Path:**
1. Recruiter encounters client issue beyond scope
2. Recruiter documents issue in CRM
3. Recruiter notifies AM via Slack/email
4. AM takes ownership and communicates with client
5. AM coordinates resolution and updates recruiter
6. Recruiter executes on resolution plan

**AM → Recruiter Direction Flow:**
1. AM learns of new client priority/preference
2. AM updates client profile in system
3. AM briefs assigned recruiters in team meeting
4. Recruiters adjust sourcing strategy accordingly
5. AM monitors execution and provides feedback

## Key Performance Indicators

### Primary KPIs (Bonus Eligible)

#### 1. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

**Definition:** Revenue retained from existing clients, including expansions and contractions.

**Calculation:**
```
NRR = (Starting ARR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting ARR × 100
```

**Target:** 110-120% annually (industry-leading)
**Acceptable:** 100-110%
**At-Risk:** <100%

**Example:**
- Starting ARR: $1M
- Expansions: +$200K (new divisions)
- Contractions: -$50K (downsizing)
- Churn: -$100K (lost client)
- NRR = ($1M + $200K - $50K - $100K) / $1M = 105%

#### 2. Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)

**Definition:** Revenue retained excluding expansion (pure retention).

**Calculation:**
```
GRR = (Starting ARR - Churn - Contraction) / Starting ARR × 100
```

**Target:** >95%
**Acceptable:** 90-95%
**At-Risk:** <90%

#### 3. Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Definition:** Client likelihood to recommend InTime (0-10 scale).

**Scoring:**
- Promoters: 9-10
- Passives: 7-8
- Detractors: 0-6
- NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors

**Target:** 50+ (excellent)
**Acceptable:** 30-50 (good)
**At-Risk:** <30 (improvement needed)

**Survey Cadence:** Quarterly (transactional), annually (relationship)

#### 4. Expansion Revenue

**Definition:** New revenue from existing clients (upsells, cross-sells).

**Target:** 15-20% of portfolio ARR annually
**Acceptable:** 10-15%
**At-Risk:** <10%

**Expansion Types:**
- New divisions/departments
- Additional job families
- Geographic expansion
- Service line addition (contract → contract-to-hire)
- Volume increases

#### 5. Churn Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of clients/revenue lost in period.

**Logo Churn Target:** <10% annually
**Revenue Churn Target:** <8% annually

**Churn Categories:**
- Voluntary (client-initiated)
- Involuntary (company-initiated, e.g., non-payment)
- Structural (client M&A, bankruptcy)

### Secondary KPIs (Performance Management)

#### 6. Client Health Score

**Composite Score (0-100):**
- Hiring velocity: 25 points
- Payment promptness: 20 points
- NPS/satisfaction: 20 points
- Executive engagement: 15 points
- Expansion pipeline: 10 points
- Issue frequency: 10 points

**Distribution Target:**
- Green (80-100): 70% of portfolio
- Yellow (50-79): 25% of portfolio
- Red (<50): <5% of portfolio

#### 7. QBR Completion Rate

**Target:** 100% of strategic clients quarterly
**Acceptable:** 90%+

**Quality Criteria:**
- Executive attendance achieved
- Metrics deck prepared 3 days in advance
- Action items documented and tracked
- Follow-up completed within 1 week

#### 8. Escalation Resolution Time

**Definition:** Time from escalation creation to resolution.

**Targets by Severity:**
- Critical (P0): <4 hours to response, <24 hours to resolution
- High (P1): <8 hours to response, <48 hours to resolution
- Medium (P2): <24 hours to response, <1 week to resolution
- Low (P3): <48 hours to response, <2 weeks to resolution

#### 9. Response Time (Client Communications)

**Email Response Time:**
- Critical: <1 hour
- Urgent: <4 hours
- Normal: <24 hours (same business day if received by 3pm)

**Meeting Request Response:**
- <24 hours to confirm or propose alternative

#### 10. Upsell Pipeline Coverage

**Definition:** Value of qualified expansion opportunities.

**Target:** 3x quarterly expansion revenue goal
**Example:** If quarterly goal is $50K expansion, maintain $150K in pipeline

### Operational Metrics (Monitoring Only)

- **Touchpoint Frequency:** Weekly check-ins completed (target: 100%)
- **CRM Hygiene:** All interactions logged within 24 hours
- **Account Plan Currency:** Updated quarterly (target: 100%)
- **Recruiter Satisfaction:** Internal NPS from assigned recruiters (target: 8+)

## Client Portfolio Management

### Portfolio Segmentation

#### Tier 1: Strategic Accounts

**Characteristics:**
- Annual revenue: $500K+ per client
- 3+ active hiring managers
- Enterprise-level contracts (MSA)
- Multi-location or multi-division
- Executive sponsor relationship

**AM Allocation:** 8-12 clients per AM
**Touch Frequency:**
- Weekly: Operations sync
- Monthly: Strategic review
- Quarterly: Executive QBR

**AM Effort:** 50-60% of time on top 3 clients

#### Tier 2: Growth Accounts

**Characteristics:**
- Annual revenue: $150K-$500K per client
- 1-2 active hiring managers
- Strong expansion potential
- Stable hiring needs

**AM Allocation:** 15-20 clients per AM
**Touch Frequency:**
- Bi-weekly: Status check-in
- Quarterly: Business review
- Semi-annual: Strategic planning

**AM Effort:** 30-40% of time

#### Tier 3: Standard Accounts

**Characteristics:**
- Annual revenue: $50K-$150K per client
- Sporadic hiring needs
- Single point of contact
- Limited expansion potential

**AM Allocation:** 20-30 clients per AM
**Touch Frequency:**
- Monthly: Check-in (often async via email)
- Semi-annual: Performance review
- Annual: Renewal discussion

**AM Effort:** 10-15% of time

### Total Portfolio Sizing

**Optimal Portfolio:**
- 30-50 total clients per AM
- $2M-$4M total ARR per AM
- 60-70% revenue concentration in top 10 clients

**Portfolio Balance Example:**
- 10 Strategic ($3M ARR) - 75% of revenue
- 15 Growth ($750K ARR) - 19% of revenue
- 25 Standard ($250K ARR) - 6% of revenue
- **Total:** 50 clients, $4M ARR

### Account Assignment Criteria

**Assignment Factors:**
1. Geographic location (time zone alignment)
2. Industry vertical expertise
3. Client size and complexity
4. Existing relationship (if transition)
5. AM capacity and tenure

**Handoff Process (Sales → AM):**
1. Sales completes deal close
2. Sales creates account profile in CRM
3. Sales schedules handoff call (Sales, AM, client)
4. AM shadows final sales call if possible
5. AM receives comprehensive account brief
6. AM sends introduction email within 24 hours
7. AM schedules kickoff call within 1 week

## Career Progression

### Account Manager Career Path

```
Junior Account Manager (0-2 years experience)
    ↓
Account Manager (2-5 years experience)
    ↓
Senior Account Manager (5-8 years experience)
    ↓
Strategic Account Manager / Enterprise AM (8+ years)
    ↓
Path A: Individual Contributor Leadership
    → Principal Account Manager
    → Named Account Executive (Fortune 500 clients)

Path B: People Management
    → Account Management Team Lead
    → Director of Client Success
    → VP of Client Success

Path C: Revenue Operations
    → Director of Revenue Operations
    → VP of Sales Operations
```

### Promotion Criteria: AM → Senior AM

**Tenure:** Minimum 2 years as AM
**Performance:**
- 6+ consecutive quarters meeting/exceeding goals
- NRR >115% over trailing 12 months
- NPS >60 portfolio average
- Zero client losses due to AM performance

**Portfolio:**
- Manage $3M+ ARR portfolio
- 3+ strategic (Tier 1) accounts
- Proven expansion success ($300K+ in upsells)

**Leadership:**
- Mentor junior AMs
- Lead process improvement initiative
- Present best practices to team

## International Considerations

### Multi-Region Account Management

#### Time Zone Management

**Global Account Coverage Model:**

**Americas (EST/PST):**
- Primary AM: US-based
- Coverage hours: 6am-6pm local time
- Handoff to EMEA for urgent escalations outside hours

**EMEA (GMT/CET):**
- Primary AM: Europe-based
- Coverage hours: 8am-6pm local time
- Weekend on-call rotation (shared with Americas)

**APAC (SGT/IST):**
- Primary AM: Asia-based
- Coverage hours: 8am-6pm local time
- Escalation to Americas leadership for executive issues

**Follow-the-Sun Model (Enterprise Clients):**
- Tier 1 clients with global operations
- 24/5 coverage via AM handoffs
- Shared CRM notes for seamless transitions
- Weekly global AM sync meeting

#### Cultural Communication Adaptations

**United States:**
- Direct, results-oriented communication
- Fast decision-making expectations
- Informal tone acceptable after rapport built
- Phone calls and video preferred over email

**United Kingdom:**
- Polite, diplomatic communication style
- Appreciate self-deprecating humor
- Email preferred for formal communications
- Punctuality highly valued

**Germany:**
- Extremely direct, factual communication
- Detailed documentation expected
- Formality maintained (use titles, surnames)
- Precision and reliability critical

**India:**
- Hierarchical respect (address senior stakeholders formally)
- Relationship-building precedes business
- Indirect communication of problems (read between lines)
- Follow up verbal agreements in writing

**Japan:**
- Formal, respectful communication always
- Silence indicates contemplation, not disagreement
- Group consensus-driven decisions (slower)
- Never criticize publicly; praise publicly

**France:**
- Intellectual debate and discussion valued
- Relationship-driven business culture
- French language appreciated (even basic phrases)
- Long lunches and dinners for relationship building

**Australia:**
- Casual, egalitarian communication style
- Skeptical of overt sales tactics
- Work-life balance respected
- Humor and banter build relationships

#### Regional Pricing & Contract Considerations

**Rate Variations by Market:**

| Region | Typical Rate Premium | Drivers |
|--------|---------------------|---------|
| United States | Baseline (100%) | Largest market, competitive |
| United Kingdom | 105-110% | Higher contractor rates, IR35 compliance |
| Germany | 110-115% | Strong labor protections, works councils |
| India (GCC/Captives) | 90-95% | Lower cost of living, but premium for expat roles |
| Middle East (UAE) | 115-125% | Tax-free salaries, hardship premium |
| Singapore | 110-120% | High cost of living, regional hub premium |

**Contract Complexity by Region:**

**North America:**
- Standard MSA with right-to-hire clauses
- At-will employment considerations
- SOW-based project work common

**European Union:**
- Works council notifications required
- Temporary worker protections (equal pay)
- Data privacy (GDPR) addendums mandatory
- Country-specific social charges

**APAC:**
- Localized employment law variations
- Gratuity/severance requirements
- Visa and work permit coordination
- Public holiday calendars vary significantly

#### Multi-Currency Revenue Tracking

**Billing Currency Options:**
- Invoice in client local currency (reduce FX risk for client)
- Invoice in USD/EUR (reduce FX risk for InTime)
- Hybrid: Large contracts with FX clauses

**NRR Calculation Adjustment:**
- Use constant currency (baseline FX rate) for apples-to-apples comparison
- Track reported NRR (actual) and constant currency NRR separately
- Example: If USD strengthens 10% vs EUR, may show churn in EUR accounts despite flat revenue

**FX Risk Management:**
- Contracts >$500K: Include annual rate adjustment clause tied to FX
- Review rates quarterly if FX moves >5%
- Hedge large multi-year contracts (Treasury function)

#### Global Compliance Considerations

**Data Residency:**
- GDPR (EU): Candidate data must be stored in EU or adequate jurisdiction
- CCPA (California): Strict data privacy rights
- PDPA (Singapore): Personal data protection requirements
- Inform clients of data storage locations

**Tax & Employment Law:**
- Permanent Establishment (PE) risk: AMs should not create taxable presence
- Avoid hiring authority or signatory power in foreign jurisdictions
- Co-employment risk management (especially in restrictive countries)
- Consult legal before agreeing to client-specific legal terms

**Trade Compliance:**
- Sanctions screening (OFAC, EU sanctions lists)
- Export control (ITAR, EAR) for defense/aerospace clients
- Anti-bribery training (FCPA, UK Bribery Act)

#### Language Considerations

**Multilingual AM Requirements:**

**Tier 1 Markets (English Sufficient):**
- US, UK, Canada, Australia, Ireland, Singapore

**Tier 2 Markets (Local Language Preferred):**
- France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Netherlands
- **Recommendation:** Native or fluent local AM

**Tier 3 Markets (Local Language Required):**
- Japan, Korea, China (Mandarin)
- Latin America (Spanish/Portuguese)
- **Requirement:** Bilingual AM or local AM with English proficiency

**Translation Support:**
- Legal documents: Professional translation required
- Marketing materials: Localized versions provided
- QBR presentations: Offer in local language for executive audience
- Email: Use professional tone, avoid idioms/slang

## Appendices

### Appendix A: Account Manager vs Sales Role

| Aspect | Sales (Hunter) | Account Manager (Farmer) |
|--------|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Primary Goal** | New client acquisition | Existing client retention & growth |
| **Compensation** | Higher variable (60/40 or 50/50) | Lower variable (70/30 or 80/20) |
| **Time Horizon** | Short-term (close deals) | Long-term (LTV maximization) |
| **Relationship Depth** | Wide (many prospects) | Deep (fewer clients) |
| **Metrics** | New ARR, win rate | NRR, churn, NPS |
| **Client Lifecycle** | Pre-sale, close | Post-sale, renewal |

**When AM may "Hunt" (Expansion Selling):**
- Selling into new divisions of existing clients
- Upselling new service lines
- Cross-selling complementary services
- These activities still count as "retention" not "new logo"

### Appendix B: Escalation Severity Matrix

| Level | Description | Response SLA | Resolution SLA | AM Involvement |
|-------|-------------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| **P0 - Critical** | Client production halt, legal threat | <1 hour | <4 hours | Immediate ownership |
| **P1 - High** | Major quality issue, executive complaint | <4 hours | <24 hours | Direct management |
| **P2 - Medium** | Process delay, minor dissatisfaction | <24 hours | <1 week | Coordinate resolution |
| **P3 - Low** | Feature request, general inquiry | <48 hours | <2 weeks | Monitor, delegate |

**Payment Promptness (20 points):**
- 20: Pay <15 days
- 15: Pay 15-30 days
- 10: Pay 30-45 days
- 5: Pay 45-60 days
- 0: >60 days or collections involvement

**NPS/Satisfaction (20 points):**
- 20: NPS 9-10 (promoter)
- 15: NPS 7-8 (passive)
