# UC-HR-008: Benefits Administration

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-30
**Role:** HR Manager
**Status:** Approved

---

## 1. Overview

This use case covers the complete benefits administration lifecycle for InTime OS employees, including enrollment, changes, COBRA administration, leave management, and benefits compliance. HR Manager ensures all employees receive appropriate benefits and maintains regulatory compliance.

**Critical Focus:** Benefits are a major employee retention tool and compliance requirement. Errors can result in penalties, employee dissatisfaction, and legal liability.

---

## 2. Actors

- **Primary:** HR Manager
- **Secondary:** Finance Team, Benefits Broker, Payroll Administrator
- **System:** Benefits Portal, HRIS, Payroll Integration, Compliance Tracker
- **External:** Insurance Carriers, 401k Provider, COBRA Administrator, State Agencies

---

## 3. Preconditions

1. HR Manager logged in with full permissions
2. Employee records exist in HRIS
3. Benefits plans configured in system
4. Carrier integrations active
5. Current plan year defined

---

## 4. Trigger

- New hire (benefits enrollment)
- Qualifying life event (marriage, birth, divorce)
- Annual open enrollment period
- Termination (COBRA eligibility)
- Employee requests benefits change
- Plan year renewal
- Compliance audit

## 6. Benefits Plan Types

### 6.1 Medical Insurance

| Plan Type | Description | Typical Use Case |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| **PPO (Preferred Provider)** | Flexibility to see any provider, lower cost in-network | Employees who want choice |
| **HDHP (High Deductible)** | Lower premiums, higher deductibles, HSA-eligible | Healthy employees, HSA savers |
| **HMO (Health Maintenance)** | Lower cost, must use network, PCP required | Cost-conscious employees |
| **EPO (Exclusive Provider)** | Network-only, no PCP required | Balance of cost and flexibility |

**Key Metrics:**
- Deductible: Amount paid before insurance kicks in
- Out-of-pocket max: Maximum annual cost to employee
- Coinsurance: Percentage split (e.g., 80/20 = insurance pays 80%)
- Copay: Fixed cost per visit/service

---

### 6.2 Health Savings Account (HSA)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Eligibility** | Must be enrolled in HDHP |
| **Employee Contribution** | $4,150/year individual, $8,300/year family (2024) |
| **Employer Contribution** | Optional (InTime: $500/year) |
| **Tax Advantage** | Triple tax benefit: pre-tax contribution, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawal for medical |
| **Portability** | Employee owns account (keeps if leaves company) |
| **Investment** | Can invest balance (like 401k) |

---

### 6.3 Dental Insurance

| Coverage Level | Typical Coverage | Annual Max |
|----------------|------------------|------------|
| **Preventive** | Cleanings, exams, X-rays | 100% (no deductible) |
| **Basic** | Fillings, extractions | 80% after $50 deductible |
| **Major** | Crowns, bridges, dentures | 50% after deductible |
| **Orthodontia** | Braces (if covered) | 50%, separate lifetime max |

---

### 6.4 Vision Insurance

| Service | Typical Coverage | Frequency |
|---------|------------------|-----------|
| **Eye Exam** | $10-20 copay | Annual |
| **Lenses** | Covered in full | Annual |
| **Frames** | $150-200 allowance | Every 2 years |
| **Contacts** | $150-200 allowance | Annual (in lieu of glasses) |
| **LASIK** | Discounted rates | As needed |

---

### 6.5 401(k) Retirement Plan

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Eligibility** | Immediate (InTime policy) |
| **Employee Contribution** | 1-100% of salary, up to IRS limit ($23,000 in 2024) |
| **Employer Match** | 4% of salary (100% match on first 4%) |
| **Vesting** | Immediate (100% vested) |
| **Catch-up (age 50+)** | Additional $7,500/year |
| **Loan Option** | Up to 50% of balance, max $50,000 |
| **Hardship Withdrawal** | Allowed for qualifying events (with penalty) |

**Investment Options:**
- Target-date funds
- Index funds (S&P 500, international, bonds)
- Individual stocks (self-directed)
- Company stock (if public)

## 7. Alternative Flows

### 7.1 Employee Waives Coverage

**Trigger:** Employee has coverage from another source (spouse, parent)

**Flow:**
1. Employee selects "Waive Coverage"
2. System requires proof of other coverage (upload document)
3. HR reviews proof (insurance card, coverage letter)
4. If approved:
   - Employee marked as "Waived - Other Coverage"
   - Annual waiver form signed
   - Can re-enroll at next open enrollment or QLE
5. If denied:
   - Employee must select a plan (required for full-time)
   - Deadline extended (one-time, 7 days)

---

### 7.2 Mid-Year Plan Change (Without QLE)

**Trigger:** Employee wants to change plans mid-year

**Rule:** Generally NOT allowed except during open enrollment or with QLE

**Exceptions:**
- Significant plan cost increase (>10%)
- Plan discontinued by carrier
- HIPAA special enrollment (loss of Medicaid/CHIP)
- Move to new coverage area

**Flow:**
1. Employee requests change
2. HR reviews for qualifying exception
3. If qualified: Process as QLE
4. If not qualified: Deny request, explain open enrollment timeline

---

### 7.3 COBRA Non-Payment

**Trigger:** COBRA participant misses payment (45-day grace period)

**Flow:**
1. System alerts HR on day 15 (payment overdue)
2. HR sends payment reminder to participant
3. Day 30: Second reminder (urgent)
4. Day 45: Coverage terminated retroactively
5. Termination notice sent to participant and carrier
6. Participant responsible for any claims during non-payment period
7. Cannot re-enroll (COBRA is one-time election)

---

### 7.4 Benefits Reconciliation (Monthly)

**Trigger:** End of month

**Flow:**
1. System generates benefits reconciliation report
2. Compare enrolled employees vs carrier records
3. Identify discrepancies:
   - Employees on system not on carrier (ADD)
   - Employees on carrier not on system (REMOVE)
   - Mismatched coverage levels
4. Generate carrier adjustment file
5. Upload to carriers by 10th of month
6. Verify carrier confirmations
7. Update payroll deductions if needed

## 12. Compliance Requirements

### 12.1 ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)

**Requirements:**
- Provide Summary Plan Description (SPD) to employees within 90 days of enrollment
- File Form 5500 annually (if 100+ employees)
- Maintain plan documents
- Offer claims appeal process

**Penalties:** Up to $110 per day for SPD violations

---

### 12.2 ACA (Affordable Care Act)

**Requirements:**
- Offer coverage to 95% of full-time employees (30+ hours/week)
- Coverage must be "affordable" (<9.12% of W-2 wages for employee-only)
- Coverage must meet "minimum value" (60% actuarial value)
- Provide 1095-C to employees by March 31
- File 1094-C and 1095-C with IRS by March 31

**Penalties:**
- $2,970 per full-time employee if no coverage offered (2024)
- $4,460 per employee who gets marketplace subsidy if coverage not affordable

---

### 12.3 HIPAA (Privacy)

**Requirements:**
- Protect employee health information (PHI)
- Provide Notice of Privacy Practices
- Obtain authorization for disclosures
- Business Associate Agreements with carriers

**Penalties:** Up to $1.5M per year for violations

---

### 12.4 COBRA

**Requirements:**
- Notify employees of COBRA rights at hire
- Notify of COBRA rights within 30 days of qualifying event
- Allow 60 days for election
- Allow 45 days for payment after election
- Provide 18-36 months continuation (based on event)

**Penalties:** Up to $110 per day per violation (DOL), plus lawsuits

---

### 12.5 Section 125 (Cafeteria Plan)

**Requirements:**
- Written plan document
- Cannot discriminate in favor of highly compensated
- Non-discrimination testing annually
- Allow mid-year changes only for QLEs

**Penalties:** Loss of pre-tax status, taxable income to employees
