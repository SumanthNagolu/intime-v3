## Role Definition

The **TA Specialist** is a COMBINED ROLE that serves as the business development and talent pipeline engine for InTime OS. Unlike Technical Recruiters (who fill client positions) or Bench Sales (who market consultants), TA Specialists focus on generating new business opportunities and building talent pools.

### Combined Responsibilities

| Traditional Role | Responsibilities in Combined Role |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Lead Generator** | Generate leads for bench sales (corporate training, staffing needs) |
| **Training Coordinator** | Source candidates for Academy training programs, manage enrollments |
| **Internal Recruiter** | Handle internal hiring for InTime positions, build company team |
| **Pipeline Manager** | Nurture leads through sales funnel, convert to deals |

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Lead Generation & Qualification
- Prospect for corporate training opportunities
- Identify companies with staffing needs
- Qualify leads using BANT framework (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- Convert leads to deals
- Maintain 3-month forward pipeline

### 2. Training Coordination (Academy)
- Source candidates for training programs (bootcamps, upskilling)
- Screen candidates for program fit (aptitude, commitment)
- Manage enrollment process from application to start
- Track training completion and post-training placement
- Build referral networks with universities and bootcamps

### 3. Internal Recruiting
- Post internal positions (recruiters, sales, operations)
- Source candidates for InTime roles
- Conduct initial screens, coordinate hiring manager interviews
- Extend offers, manage negotiation
- Coordinate onboarding with HR and IT

### 4. Pipeline Management
- Track leads through qualification stages
- Nurture long-term prospects
- Hand off qualified deals to Sales or Account Managers
- Maintain CRM hygiene and forecasting accuracy
- Report on pipeline health weekly

### 5. Outreach & Campaigns
- Create email campaigns for lead generation
- Run LinkedIn outreach campaigns
- Manage prospect lists and segmentation
- Track campaign performance and optimize
- A/B test messaging and offers

### Metric Definitions

**Qualified Lead Criteria:**
- Budget confirmed (or budget range identified)
- Decision maker identified and engaged
- Clear need articulated
- Timeline defined (within 6 months)

**Deal Types:**
- **Training Deal** - Company pays for employee training
- **Staffing Deal** - Company needs contractors/consultants
- **Partnership Deal** - Referral or co-marketing agreement

## Daily Workflow Summary

### Morning Routine (8:00 AM - 10:00 AM)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. Review Dashboard (10 min)                                    │
│    ✓ New inbound leads overnight                                │
│    ✓ Deal stage changes needed today                            │
│    ✓ Overdue follow-ups                                         │
│    ✓ Training applications pending review                       │
│                                                                  │
│ 2. Check Email & LinkedIn (15 min)                              │
│    ✓ Respond to prospect inquiries                              │
│    ✓ Review LinkedIn connection requests                        │
│    ✓ Check campaign responses                                   │
│                                                                  │
│ 3. Prioritize Day (5 min)                                       │
│    ✓ Top 3 deals to move forward                                │
│    ✓ Critical follow-ups                                        │
│    ✓ Training interviews scheduled                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Mid-Morning (10:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

- **Lead Generation Activities:**
  - LinkedIn prospecting (30-50 profile views)
  - Outbound calls to warm leads (5-10 calls)
  - Email outreach (20-30 personalized emails)
  - Research companies in target verticals

### Afternoon (12:00 PM - 3:00 PM)

- **Discovery & Qualification:**
  - Discovery calls with new leads (2-3 calls)
  - Training candidate screening interviews (1-2 interviews)
  - Internal candidate phone screens (1-2 interviews)
  - Update CRM with call notes

### Late Afternoon (3:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

- **Pipeline Management:**
  - Update deal stages and next steps
  - Send follow-up emails
  - Prepare proposals or training packages
  - Log all activities in CRM
  - Plan next day priorities
  - Weekly pipeline review (Fridays)

## Training Requirements

Before using the system, a new TA Specialist should complete:

### 1. System Orientation (1 hour)
- Navigation and UI overview
- Keyboard shortcuts and command bar
- Dashboard walkthrough
- Entity relationships (Leads → Deals → Jobs)

### 2. Lead Management Training (2 hours)
- Lead generation strategies
- BANT qualification framework
- Lead scoring and prioritization
- CRM hygiene and data entry
- Handoff to Sales process

### 3. Deal Pipeline Training (2 hours)
- Deal stages and progression
- Forecasting accuracy
- Win/loss analysis
- RACI ownership model
- Commission structure

### 4. Academy/Training Coordination (3 hours)
- Training program overview
- Candidate screening criteria
- Enrollment process end-to-end
- Post-training placement tracking
- Referral network building

### 5. Internal Hiring Training (2 hours)
- Internal job posting workflow
- Candidate sourcing strategies
- Interview coordination
- Offer management and negotiation
- Onboarding handoff to HR

### 6. Campaign Management (2 hours)
- Email campaign builder
- LinkedIn campaign setup
- A/B testing methodology
- Analytics interpretation
- Deliverability best practices

### 7. Sales Methodology (2 hours)
- Consultative selling approach
- Discovery question framework (SPIN)
- Objection handling
- Value-based positioning
- Closing techniques

### 8. Compliance Training (1 hour)
- EEOC compliance for internal hiring
- GDPR/CCPA data privacy
- Email marketing regulations (CAN-SPAM)
- LinkedIn terms of service
- Anti-spam policies

## Success Criteria

A TA Specialist is successful when:

### Lead Generation
- ✅ 15-20 qualified leads generated per month
- ✅ Lead quality score >75% (based on BANT)
- ✅ Response rate >15% on outreach campaigns
- ✅ Cost per lead <$50

### Deal Conversion
- ✅ Lead-to-Deal conversion rate >25%
- ✅ Deal closure rate >20%
- ✅ Average deal size >$15K
- ✅ Sales cycle <60 days

### Training Coordination
- ✅ 5-8 enrollments per quarter
- ✅ Training completion rate >80%
- ✅ Post-training placement rate >70% within 90 days
- ✅ Candidate satisfaction >4.5/5

### Internal Hiring
- ✅ 1-2 internal positions filled per quarter
- ✅ Time to fill <30 days
- ✅ 90-day retention >90%
- ✅ Hiring manager satisfaction >4.5/5

### Pipeline Health
- ✅ 3-month forward pipeline value >$150K
- ✅ Pipeline velocity <60 days average
- ✅ Forecast accuracy >85%
- ✅ CRM data quality >95%

## Key Business Rules

### 1. Lead Ownership
- TA Specialist is Responsible (R) for all self-generated leads
- Inbound leads assigned round-robin or by territory
- Lead ownership can be transferred with manager approval
- Leads inactive >90 days auto-moved to nurture campaign

### 2. Deal Handoff
- Training deals: TA Specialist owns through completion
- Staffing deals >$100K: Hand off to Sales Rep at proposal stage
- TA Specialist remains Consulted (C) after handoff
- Commission split: 50% to TA Specialist on handoff deals

### 3. Training Enrollment
- All enrollments require TA Specialist screening
- Deposit required before enrollment confirmed
- Refund policy: 100% within 7 days, 50% within 14 days, 0% after
- Post-training placement tracking mandatory for 90 days

### 4. Internal Hiring
- All internal jobs require TA Manager approval before posting
- Offer approval: HR Manager (always), CEO (if >$100K base)
- Referral bonus: $1K-$2K per hire (paid at 90 days)
- 90-day retention tracked and impacts TA Specialist bonus

### 5. Campaign Management
- All campaigns require CAN-SPAM compliance
- LinkedIn campaigns: Max 100 connection requests/week
- A/B testing: Required for campaigns >500 recipients
- Campaign performance reviewed monthly, low performers paused
