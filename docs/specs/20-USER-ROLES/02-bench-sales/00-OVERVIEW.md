## 1. Overview

The **Bench Sales Recruiter** is a combined Individual Contributor (IC) role in InTime OS that manages the complete lifecycle of consultant bench operations. This role combines five traditional specialized roles into one end-to-end position: Bench Builder, Marketing Specialist, Vendor Relations Manager, Immigration Coordinator, and Placement Specialist.

**Core Mission:** Maximize bench consultant utilization by sourcing quality consultants, marketing them effectively, managing vendor relationships, ensuring immigration compliance, and closing placements quickly to minimize days on bench.

---

## 2. Actors

### 2.1 Primary Actor
- **Bench Sales Recruiter** - End-to-end owner of bench consultant lifecycle

### 2.2 Secondary Actors
- **Bench Sales Manager** - Pod manager, approves key decisions
- **Vendor Partners** - Third-party staffing agencies providing consultant bench access
- **Bench Consultants** - Internal consultants on the bench (not on billable projects)
- **HR/Immigration** - Handles visa compliance, work authorization
- **Finance** - Processes vendor payments, commission calculations
- **Clients** - End clients with job requirements

### 2.3 System Actors
- **Immigration Alert System** - Automated visa expiration monitoring
- **Vendor Bench Sync** - Automated import of third-party consultant profiles
- **Hotlist Generator** - Automated marketing document creation
- **Commission Calculator** - Automated payment calculations based on custom terms

| Aspect | Traditional Recruiter | Bench Sales Recruiter |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Candidate Pool** | External candidates (not employed) | Internal consultants (already hired) |
| **Job Source** | Client job orders (direct clients) | External market jobs (vendor requirements) |

| **Marketing Focus** | Candidate presentation to clients | Mass marketing to vendor network |
| **Immigration** | Pre-screen for eligibility | Active case management and tracking |

## 8. Business Rules

### 8.1 Bench Definition & Thresholds

**Bench Definition:**
- **On Bench** = Consultant not currently on a billable client project
- **Bench Start Date** = Last project end date OR hire date if never placed

- **Bench Status** = Automated based on days on bench

### 8.2 Submission Rules

**Submission Limits:**
- Maximum **3 active submissions** per consultant at a time
- Cannot submit to same vendor twice within **30 days** (unless explicitly requested by vendor)
- Cannot submit to same requirement twice (duplicate prevention)
- Must contact consultant and receive confirmation before each submission

**Submission Requirements:**
- âœ… Valid work authorization (>180 days validity or pending renewal)
- âœ… Current resume (updated within 30 days)
- âœ… Consultant consent documented
- âœ… Rate approval (if outside standard margin)
- âœ… Skills match requirement (>70% match score)

**Response SLA:**
- 24-hour response to vendor inquiries
- 4-hour response to interview requests
- Same-day feedback after interviews

### 8.3 Hotlist Rules

**Hotlist Standards:**
- Minimum **5 consultants** per hotlist
- Maximum **25 consultants** per hotlist
- Must update all consultant profiles before hotlist creation (freshen data)
- Hotlist expires after **14 days** (auto-archive)
- Track engagement: opens, clicks, responses, submissions

**Hotlist Distribution:**
- Require opt-in from vendors (CAN-SPAM compliance)
- Maximum **3 hotlists per week** to same vendor
- Personalize introduction (no generic blasts)
- Include clear CTA (call to action)

### 8.4 Immigration Compliance Rules

**Work Authorization Validity:**

- H1B transfer requires current valid H1B (not expired)

### 8.6 Contract Type Rules

**C2C (Corp-to-Corp):**
- Consultant has own legal entity (LLC, Corp, S-Corp)
- InTime pays consultant's company
- Consultant responsible for own taxes (1099)
- No benefits, no payroll taxes
- Typically higher bill rate (consultant covers overhead)

**W2 (Employee):**
- Consultant is InTime employee
- InTime withholds taxes, provides benefits
- Payroll processing required
- Workers comp insurance applies
- Lower bill rate (InTime covers benefits, taxes)

**1099 (Independent Contractor):**
- Consultant is self-employed individual
- InTime pays consultant directly (1099 form)
- Consultant responsible for own taxes
- No benefits, no withholding
- Must meet IRS criteria (control, independence)

**Rate Implications:**

## 14. Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Cause | Solution | Prevention |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Consultant not responding** | Disengaged, seeking other opportunities | Try all channels (phone, email, text, LinkedIn). Escalate to manager after 3 attempts. | Weekly engagement calls, keep consultants informed |
| **Vendor rejecting consultant** | Skills mismatch, rate too high, visa restrictions | Document rejection reason, update consultant profile, adjust marketing. | Pre-qualify requirements, verify skills match |
| **External job expired** | Filled by another agency | Mark job as expired, remove from active matching, archive. | Check job age before submission, prioritize recent postings |
| **Visa about to expire** | Renewal not filed, processing delays | Flag in immigration dashboard, notify HR/attorney immediately, stop new submissions. | 90-day advance alerts, proactive renewals |
| **Consultant wants rate increase** | Market rate changes, skill upgrades | Discuss with manager, review market rates, update if approved. | Quarterly rate reviews, competitive analysis |
| **Multiple reps contacting consultant** | Poor assignment visibility | Check assignment in system. Consultant should have single assigned bench rep. | Clear assignment rules, dashboard visibility |
| **No external jobs matching skills** | Niche skills, market slowdown | Expand search to adjacent skills, consider training/upskilling, partner with specialized vendors. | Diversify consultant skills, cross-training |
| **Hotlist low engagement** | Poor targeting, stale data | Segment vendors by vertical, refresh consultant data, A/B test subject lines. | Targeted marketing, data quality |
| **Vendor slow to respond** | High volume, relationship issues | Follow up via phone, escalate to vendor account manager. | Build stronger relationships, VIP status |
| **Immigration case stuck** | RFE, processing delays | Contact attorney for status, consider premium processing if available. | Premium processing for critical cases |
| **Consultant fails interview** | Preparation issues, skills gap | Debrief consultant, identify gaps, provide training resources. | Pre-interview prep calls, mock interviews |
| **Placement falls through** | Background check, drug test, client change of mind | Document reason, keep consultant warm, find backup opportunities. | Manage expectations, backup plans |
| **Commission dispute with vendor** | Unclear terms, invoice mismatch | Review vendor agreement, involve Finance, escalate to manager if needed. | Clear terms documentation, invoice verification |
| **Client requests rate reduction** | Budget cuts, market competition | Negotiate with manager approval, consider volume discounts, adjust scope. | Lock rates in contracts, cost-of-living clauses |
