## 1. Role Overview

The **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** is responsible for setting the strategic vision, driving company growth, managing board and investor relations, and serving as the ultimate escalation point for critical decisions. The CEO focuses on long-term strategy (3-5 years), market positioning, and building a world-class organization.

**Core Identity:** Strategic visionary and external face of the company.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Role ID | `ceo` |
| Role Type | Executive Leadership |
| Reports To | Board of Directors |
| Direct Reports | COO, CFO, Admin (functional) |
| Primary Entities | OKRs, Strategic Accounts, Board Reports, Partnerships |
| RACI Default | **Accountable (A)** for company-wide strategic decisions |
| Key Focus | Vision & Strategy |
| Time Horizon | 3-5 years |
| Key Question | "Where are we going and why?" |

## 2. Key Responsibilities

### 2.1 Vision & Strategy

- **Strategic Vision:** Set and communicate 3-5 year company vision and strategy
- **OKR Ownership:** Define company-level OKRs and track strategic initiatives
- **Market Positioning:** Position company competitively in target markets
- **Growth Strategy:** Drive revenue growth, market expansion, and valuation
- **Innovation:** Identify and pursue strategic innovations and opportunities

### 2.2 Board & Investor Relations

- **Board Management:** Prepare and deliver quarterly board presentations
- **Investor Relations:** Manage relationships with investors and stakeholders
- **Fundraising:** Lead fundraising efforts (Series A, B, C, etc.)
- **Governance:** Ensure proper corporate governance and compliance
- **Strategic Communication:** Communicate vision to all stakeholders

### 2.3 External Relations

- **Brand & PR:** Own company brand positioning and public relations
- **Strategic Partnerships:** Negotiate major partnerships and alliances
- **Industry Leadership:** Represent company at industry events and forums
- **Key Client Relationships:** Maintain relationships with strategic accounts
- **M&A Strategy:** Identify and evaluate acquisition opportunities

### 2.4 Executive Leadership

- **Leadership Team:** Build and lead world-class executive team
- **Culture:** Define and champion company culture and values
- **Talent Strategy:** Attract and retain top executive talent
- **Succession Planning:** Develop succession plans for key roles
- **Ultimate Escalation:** Serve as final decision-maker for critical issues

### 2.5 Strategic Oversight

- **Financial Oversight:** Review financial performance with CFO
- **Operational Oversight:** Review operational metrics with COO
- **Risk Management:** Identify and mitigate strategic risks
- **Compliance:** Ensure company-wide regulatory compliance
- **Crisis Management:** Lead response to company-wide crises

## 5. Daily Workflow

### 5.1 Morning Routine (7:00 AM - 9:00 AM)

```
7:00 - Review strategic KPIs and overnight developments
7:15 - Scan news for industry updates and competitive moves
7:30 - Review critical escalations and board-level items
7:45 - Prepare for morning meetings
8:00 - Leadership team standup (COO, CFO, key VPs)
8:30 - Review calendar and prioritize day
```

### 5.2 Mid-Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

```
9:00  - Strategic meetings with leadership team
10:00 - External stakeholder calls (investors, partners)
11:00 - Board member 1:1s (as scheduled)
11:30 - Strategic account relationship calls
```

### 5.3 Afternoon (12:00 PM - 3:00 PM)

```
12:00 - Lunch meetings with key clients or partners
1:00  - Deep work: Strategic planning, board prep
2:00  - Cross-functional strategic reviews
2:30  - OKR progress reviews
```

### 5.4 Late Afternoon (3:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

```
3:00  - CEO Staff meeting or executive 1:1s
4:00  - Industry events, speaking engagements
5:00  - End-of-day review with COO
5:30  - Market research and competitive analysis
```

### 5.5 Weekly Rhythms

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| Monday | Week planning, leadership team meeting |
| Tuesday | External meetings (investors, partners) |
| Wednesday | Strategic account reviews |
| Thursday | OKR reviews, strategic initiatives |
| Friday | Board prep, reflection, team recognition |

### 5.6 Monthly Rhythms

|------|-------|

## 11. Distinction from Other Roles

### 11.1 CEO vs. COO

| Aspect | CEO | COO |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Focus | Vision (Where) | Execution (How) |
| Time Horizon | 3-5 years | Daily to Quarterly |
| Primary Stakeholder | Board, Investors | Teams, Pods |
| Key Metric | Market Share, Valuation | Efficiency, SLA |
| Decision Type | Strategic | Operational |
| Notifications | Strategic alerts | INFORMED on ALL changes |
| External Focus | Investors, Partners | Clients, Vendors |

### 11.2 CEO vs. CFO

| Aspect | CEO | CFO |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Focus | Strategy | Finance |
| Primary Entities | OKRs, Partnerships | Invoices, Revenue |
| Key Metric | Growth, Valuation | Margin, DSO |
| Board Role | Lead presenter | Financial section |
| Approval Role | Strategic decisions | Financial approvals |
| External Focus | Investors, Media | Auditors, Banks |

### 11.3 CEO vs. Board

| Aspect | CEO | Board |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Role | Execute strategy | Govern and advise |
| Accountability | To Board | To Shareholders |
| Time Commitment | Full-time | Part-time |
| Decision Authority | Day-to-day | Major decisions |
| Hiring | Executive team | CEO |

## 12. Success Criteria

### 12.1 Strategic Leadership

- Revenue growth: 25%+ YoY sustained
- Market position: Top 3 in target markets
- Valuation growth: 30%+ annually
- OKR achievement: 80%+ quarterly

### 12.2 Stakeholder Relations

- Board satisfaction: 9/10 rating
- Investor confidence: Successful fundraising rounds
- Client NPS: 70+ sustained
- Employee NPS: 70+ sustained

### 12.3 External Impact

- Industry recognition: Speaking engagements, awards
- Media presence: Positive press coverage
- Partnership success: Strategic alliances formed
