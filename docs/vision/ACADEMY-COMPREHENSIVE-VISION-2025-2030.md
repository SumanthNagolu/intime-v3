## 1. The Manifesto: Why We Exist

### The "Education-Employment" Gap
The modern technical education system is fundamentally broken.
- **Universities** teach theory, not practice.
- **Bootcamps** churn out generalists into saturated markets (React/Node.js).
- **Corporate Training** is passive, boring, and ineffective ("watch video, take quiz").

**The Result:** A chronic shortage of specialized talent in high-value industries like Insurance Technology (Guidewire), despite millions of underemployed graduates.

### The InTime Solution
InTime Academy is **not a school**. It is a **Talent Manufacturing Engine**.
We do not sell courses; we sell **Career Transformations**.

**Our Core Promise:**
> **"We take aptitude. We add precision training. We guarantee a career."**

## 3. The Product: Precision Education

### A. The Niche: Guidewire (Blue Ocean)
We ignore the "Red Ocean" of web development. We focus exclusively on **Guidewire PolicyCenter, BillingCenter, and ClaimCenter**.
- **High Demand:** Every major insurer runs on it.
- **Low Supply:** Official training is expensive ($4.5k+) and gated.
- **High Pay:** Starting salaries $85k - $110k.

### B. The Methodology: Socratic Mastery
We reject passive learning.
1.  **Socratic AI Mentor:**
    -   **Tech:** Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini.
    -   **Behavior:** Never gives the answer. Asks "Why do you think that?" or "Check the PolicyCenter schema."
    -   **Benefit:** Students learn *how to think*, not just copy-paste code.
    -   **Cost:** <$1/student/month (vs $5k/mo for human TAs).
2.  **Sequential Mastery:**
    -   You cannot see Module 2 until you ace Module 1.
    -   No "C students." You master it, or you don't move forward.
3.  **Production Simulations:**
    -   No "Todo Lists." Students build **"Pet Insurance Products,"** **"Homeowners Quoting Engines,"** and **"Claim Processing Workflows."**

### C. The Curriculum (8-Week Sprint)
-   **Weeks 1-2:** Insurance Fundamentals (The "Why") & Java/Gosu Basics.
-   **Weeks 3-4:** PolicyCenter Configuration (The "What").
-   **Weeks 5-6:** Integration & API Design (The "How").
-   **Week 7:** Real-world Project (Capstone).
-   **Week 8:** Interview Prep & Resume Polish.

## 4. Operational Architecture

### The "Pod" Structure
The Academy is run by lean, high-leverage teams called Pods.

**Academy Pod (2 Humans + AI Swarm):**
-   **1 Senior Instructor (The Expert):** Handles high-level curriculum, difficult technical blockers, and live Q&A.
-   **1 Junior Success Manager (The Guide):** Tracks motivation, deadlines, and "human" support.
-   **AI Agent Swarm:**
    -   *MentorBot:* Answers 95% of technical queries instantly.
    -   *GraderBot:* Reviews code against style guides and unit tests.
    -   *NudgeBot:* Detects students falling behind and sends motivation.

**Scalability:**
One Pod can handle **200 concurrent students** (vs 30 in traditional models) because AI handles the repetitive 80% of the workload.
