# InTime Recruiting Team Hiring Roadmap

*How to build the staffing operations team from zero to scale.*

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the phased approach to building InTime's recruiting operations team. The team grows in alignment with business capacity and revenue milestones.

---

## Phase 0: Founder-Led (Now)

### Team: 1-2 people

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  FOUNDER (wears all hats)                           │
│                                                     │
│  + Operations support (part-time/contractor)        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### What Founder Does:
- Source and qualify candidates
- Develop client relationships
- Close deals and fill jobs
- Handle operations (timesheets, payroll)
- Build the platform

### Milestones to Exit This Phase:
- 5-10 active placements
- 2-3 repeat clients
- $100K+ monthly billings
- Platform MVP operational

### Key Hire Signal:
When you're turning away business because you can't handle it alone.

### First 3 Hires (in order):

#### Hire #1: Senior Recruiter (Full-Cycle)

**Why First:** Can handle multiple functions while you build out.

**Profile:**
- 3-5 years staffing experience (IT preferred)
- Can source, screen, qualify, and close
- Has done bench sales or willing to learn
- Self-starter, needs minimal supervision
- Hustle + quality mindset

**Compensation:** $60K-$80K base + commission (20-30% of gross margin)

**Responsibilities:**
- Build the candidate pool
- Market available consultants
- Support job fulfillment
- Train future hires

**Metrics:**
- 30+ qualified candidates/month
- 80%+ bench utilization
- 2+ placements/month

---

#### Hire #2: Front Office Recruiter

**Why Second:** Founder can't fill all jobs alone.

**Profile:**
- 1-3 years recruiting experience
- Strong closer - can prep candidates and negotiate
- Detail-oriented on interview scheduling
- Good communicator with clients
- Hungry to grow

**Compensation:** $50K-$65K base + commission (15-25% of gross margin on fills)

**Responsibilities:**
- Match candidates to open jobs
- Prep candidates for interviews
- Coordinate interview scheduling
- Close candidates through offer

**Metrics:**
- < 24 hour first submission
- 70%+ fill rate
- 90%+ 90-day retention

---

#### Hire #3: Operations Coordinator (Part-time → Full-time)

**Why Third:** Operations becomes a bottleneck fast.

**Profile:**
- Detail-oriented, organized
- Experience with timesheets/payroll preferred
- Can handle compliance paperwork
- Customer service mindset for contractors
- Can be trained on systems

**Compensation:** $40K-$55K (or part-time equivalent)

**Responsibilities:**
- Timesheet collection and processing
- Payroll coordination
- Compliance documentation
- Contractor onboarding
- Basic invoicing

**Metrics:**
- 100% on-time payroll
- 99%+ accuracy
- < 4 hour response to contractor issues

---

### Founder's Role in Phase 1:
- Account management (own all client relationships)
- Business development (bring in new clients)
- Strategy and hiring
- Platform development (with tech team)

### Milestones to Exit This Phase:
- 20-30 active placements
- 5-8 active clients
- $300K+ monthly billings
- Team hitting metrics independently

## Compensation Philosophy (Recruiting Team)

### Base + Commission Structure

| Role | Base Range | Variable | Total Target |
|------|------------|----------|--------------|
| Back Office Recruiter | $50K-$70K | 20-30% of GP on placements | $70K-$100K |
| Bench Sales | $50K-$70K | 25-35% of GP on bench placements | $80K-$120K |
| Front Office Recruiter | $50K-$70K | 15-25% of GP on fills | $70K-$100K |
| Account Manager | $70K-$90K | 5-10% of GP on book | $90K-$130K |
| BD Rep | $45K-$55K | Bonus per qualified meeting + deal bonus | $60K-$80K |
| Delivery Manager | $60K-$80K | Bonus tied to retention | $70K-$95K |
| Operations Coordinator | $45K-$60K | Small bonus | $50K-$65K |
| Managers | $90K-$130K | 10-20% bonus tied to team metrics | $110K-$150K |

### Commission Structures

**Back Office/Bench Sales:**
- 20% of gross profit (spread fee) on placements they source
- Paid when cash received
- Accelerators for above-target performance

**Front Office:**
- 15% of gross profit on jobs they fill
- Higher % for tough-to-fill roles
- Bonus for retention

**Account Manager:**
- 5-10% of gross profit on their book
- Bonus for account growth
- Bonus for client retention

## Interview Process (Recruiting Roles)

### Stage 1: Resume Review (24 hours)
- Staffing experience?
- Track record of results?
- Stability?

### Stage 2: Phone Screen (30 min)
- Motivation for staffing
- Understanding of our model
- Basic fit

### Stage 3: Role Play (45-60 min)
- **Recruiter:** Mock candidate call
- **Account Manager:** Mock client discovery
- **BD:** Mock cold call
- **Ops:** Process scenario

### Stage 4: In-Person/Panel (1-2 hours)
- Meet the team
- Culture fit
- Deep dive on experience
- Scenario questions

### Stage 5: References + Offer
- 3 references (including manager)
- Background check
- Offer within 48 hours

## What to Look For

### Green Flags

- **Hustle with quality:** Works hard AND works smart
- **Ownership:** "I did X" not "we did X"
- **Resilience:** Can handle rejection
- **Curiosity:** Asks good questions
- **Coachable:** Wants feedback
- **Results-oriented:** Can cite specific metrics
- **Relationship builder:** People like them

### Red Flags

- **Job hopper without growth:** Multiple short stints
- **Blames others:** Former employers, colleagues
- **Vague on results:** Can't cite numbers
- **Entitlement:** Expects without earning
- **Doesn't do homework:** Didn't research us
- **Only cares about money:** No mission alignment
- **Bad references:** Listen carefully

## Key Questions Before Each Hire

1. **Do we have enough work?** (Don't hire ahead of demand)
2. **What's the ROI?** (Revenue or capacity they'll generate)
3. **Can we afford it?** (Runway for ramp + salary)
4. **Do we have management capacity?** (Who will train and manage?)
5. **Is this the highest priority hire?** (Opportunity cost)

## Final Notes

### Hire Slow, Fire Fast

- Take time to find the right people
- But don't keep wrong fits hoping they'll improve
- Culture is set by who you hire AND who you tolerate

### Build Culture Early

- First 5 hires define the culture
- Be explicit about values from day 1
- Lead by example

### Invest in Training

- Every hour of training saves 10 hours of mistakes
- Create playbooks and documentation
- Build a learning culture

### Measure Everything

- What gets measured gets managed
- But don't over-optimize metrics at expense of relationships
- Leading indicators (activity) AND lagging indicators (results)
