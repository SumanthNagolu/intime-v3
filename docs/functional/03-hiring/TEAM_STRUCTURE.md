## Part II: Role Definitions

### Engineering Roles

#### Junior Software Engineer (IC1)

**Level:** Entry-level, 0-2 years experience

**Responsibilities:**
- Implement well-defined features with guidance
- Write tests for new code
- Fix bugs in existing systems
- Participate in code reviews (as reviewer and reviewee)
- Learn codebase and technology stack

**Expectations:**
- Ships features with senior guidance
- Asks questions proactively
- Improves over time with feedback

**Compensation Band:** $80K - $110K + equity

---

#### Software Engineer (IC2)

**Level:** Mid-level, 2-4 years experience

**Responsibilities:**
- Implement features independently
- Own small to medium features end-to-end
- Write comprehensive tests
- Review others' code effectively
- Debug production issues
- Contribute to technical documentation

**Expectations:**
- Ships features independently
- Identifies and addresses edge cases
- Communicates blockers proactively

**Compensation Band:** $120K - $160K + equity

---

## Part III: Hiring Principles

### What We Optimize For

1. **Impact Potential** > Years of Experience
2. **Learning Velocity** > Current Knowledge
3. **Culture Contribution** > Culture Fit
4. **Ownership Mindset** > Task Completion
5. **Communication Clarity** > Technical Depth (for most roles)

### Interview Process

#### Stage 1: Application (1-2 days)
- Resume review
- Brief application questions
- Initial screen by recruiter

#### Stage 2: Phone Screen (30 min)
- Recruiter or hiring manager
- Background and motivation
- Role overview
- Mutual fit assessment

#### Stage 3: Technical/Skill Assessment (60-90 min)
- **Engineering:** Live coding or take-home
- **Product:** Case study or product critique
- **Design:** Portfolio review + design exercise
- **Sales:** Mock pitch or discovery call
- **CS:** Scenario-based role play

#### Stage 4: Team Interview (2-3 hours)
- Meet 3-4 team members
- Cross-functional perspectives
- Culture and values alignment
- Deep dive on experience

#### Stage 5: Final (30-60 min)
- Founder/executive conversation
- Big picture alignment
- Questions and close

#### Stage 6: References & Offer
- 3 references checked
- Offer extended within 48 hours

### Interview Questions We Ask

**For Everyone:**
- Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned.
- What's the most impactful thing you've accomplished?
- How do you handle disagreement with a colleague?
- What do you want to learn in the next year?
- Why InTime? Why now?

**For Engineering:**
- Walk me through how you'd design [system X].
- How do you approach debugging a complex issue?
- What's your philosophy on testing?
- Tell me about technical debt you've paid down.

**For Product:**
- How would you improve [feature Y]?
- How do you prioritize when everything is important?
- Tell me about a feature that failed and why.
- How do you handle engineering pushback on requirements?

**For Design:**
- Walk me through your design process.
- How do you handle stakeholder feedback you disagree with?
- What makes a design system effective?
- Show me something you're proud of and something you'd redo.

### Red Flags

- **Blame-shifting:** Takes no responsibility for failures
- **Know-it-all:** Can't acknowledge gaps in knowledge
- **Negative about past companies:** Likely to be negative about us
- **Vague accomplishments:** Can't articulate specific contributions
- **No questions:** Lack of curiosity about the role/company
- **Values misalignment:** Doesn't resonate with our culture

### Green Flags

- **Specific examples:** Can articulate impact with numbers
- **Ownership:** "I did X" not just "We did X"
- **Curiosity:** Asks thoughtful questions
- **Self-awareness:** Knows strengths and growth areas
- **Energy:** Genuinely excited about the problem we solve
- **Humility:** Willing to learn, open to feedback

## Part V: Org Design Principles

### Principle 1: Small, Autonomous Teams

- **Team size:** 4-8 people
- **Composition:** Cross-functional (eng, product, design)
- **Ownership:** Each team owns a domain end-to-end
- **Communication:** Within-team daily, cross-team weekly

### Principle 2: Clear Accountability

- Every initiative has one owner
- Every team has one leader
- Every metric has one person accountable
- When in doubt, fewer cooks in the kitchen

### Principle 3: Minimal Process

- Process exists to solve problems, not create work
- Add process only when pain is acute
- Remove process that no longer serves its purpose
- Bias toward guidelines over rules

### Principle 4: Communication Hygiene

- Write it down
- Share broadly
- Default to public (within company)
- Meetings are expensive—use sparingly

### Principle 5: Invest in Growth

- Managers spend 20%+ time on people development
- Budget for learning (courses, conferences)
- Internal mobility encouraged
- Promote from within when possible

## Appendix: Compensation Philosophy

### Base Salary

- **Target:** 75th percentile for role, level, and location
- **Bands:** Published internally for transparency
- **Adjustments:** Annual market rate review

### Equity

- **Philosophy:** Every employee is an owner
- **Vesting:** 4-year vest, 1-year cliff
- **Refresh grants:** For strong performers and market adjustments
- **Liquidity:** As appropriate at company milestones

### Benefits

- **Healthcare:** 100% employee, 75% dependent
- **401(k):** Match TBD
- **PTO:** Flexible (minimum 15 days encouraged)
- **Learning:** $2,000/year professional development
- **Equipment:** Latest laptop, WFH setup budget
- **Parental leave:** 12 weeks paid

### Performance & Raises

- **Review cycle:** Quarterly check-ins, annual formal review
- **Raise timing:** Annual, tied to performance and market
- **Promotion criteria:** Published ladder expectations
- **Bonus:** For exceptional performance (discretionary)
