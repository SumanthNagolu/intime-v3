## 1. Strategic HR Planning

### 1.1 Workforce Planning

**Purpose:** Align hiring and resource allocation with business phases to optimize costs and team productivity.

**Headcount Forecasting by Business Phase:**

| Phase | Period | Headcount | Leads | Interns | Monthly Burn | Status |
|-------|--------|-----------|-------|---------|--------------|--------|
| Phase 0 | Pre-launch | 1-2 | 1 | 1-2 | ₹1.5-2L | Completed |
| Phase 1 | Current | 16 | 7 | 9 | ~₹12L | Active |
| Phase 2 | 6-12 months | 30+ | 15 | 15+ | ~₹25L+ | Planned |

**Workforce Planning Responsibilities:**
- **Owner:** CEO + HR Lead
- **Frequency:** Quarterly review, monthly monitoring
- **Process:**
  1. CEO defines quarterly business objectives and hiring needs
  2. HR Lead conducts capacity assessment of current team
  3. Identify skill gaps and attrition risks
  4. Create 3-month hiring plan with role descriptions and budgets
  5. Communicate plan to leads and stakeholders
  6. Monitor hiring progress monthly

**Timeline/SLA:** Plan finalized by first week of each quarter

**Tools Used:** Google Sheets (headcount tracker), Asana (hiring board)

## 2. Employee Lifecycle

### 2.1 Pre-Boarding (Offer Acceptance to Day 1)

**Purpose:** Ensure new employees have all documentation, IT setup, and buddy support to start effectively on Day 1.

**Process Timeline:** 7-14 days before start date

**Owner:** HR Lead + Functional Manager

**Trigger:** Offer letter signed and returned by candidate

**Pre-Boarding Checklist:**

**Documentation Collection (within 24 hours of offer acceptance):**
- [ ] PAN Card (copy + original verification)
- [ ] Aadhaar Card (copy + original verification)
- [ ] Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)
- [ ] Passport size photograph (2 copies, 4x6 cm)
- [ ] Educational certificates (10th, 12th, bachelor's/master's or relevant)
- [ ] Experience letters (from all previous employers)
- [ ] Address proof (voter ID, driving license, or utility bill)
- [ ] ESI/ESIC proof (if applicable, from previous employer)
- [ ] Medical fitness certificate (optional, for on-site roles)

**Background Verification (3-7 days):**
- Verify educational qualifications with institutes
- Contact previous employers to confirm tenure and role
- Criminal background check (via third-party vendor if required)
- Address verification (if different from current residence)
- Documentation: Maintain verification report in personnel file

**IT Setup (by Day -1):**
- [ ] Laptop allocated and configured (OS, antivirus, basic software)
- [ ] Email account created (firstname@intime.co or variant)
- [ ] Slack workspace access enabled
- [ ] Google Workspace added to team Drive
- [ ] VPN/Security credentials provided (if remote)
- [ ] Communication tools installed (Zoom, Meet)
- [ ] Access to project management tools (Asana)
- [ ] Send welcome email with IT credentials and first-day schedule
- [ ] Create personnel file (physical or digital)

**Buddy Assignment (by Day -2):**
- Assign same-level or slightly senior buddy (preferably same team)
- Buddy orientation: role, introduce to team, facilities tour, first-day schedule
- Buddy contact details shared with new employee

**Documentation Template:** Pre-Boarding Checklist (saved in HR folder)

**SLA:** All pre-boarding complete 24 hours before Day 1 start

**Tools Used:** Google Forms (document collection), Gmail (communications), Google Sheets (tracking)

---

### 2.2 Onboarding (Day 1 to Day 30)

**Purpose:** Integrate new employee into company culture, clarify role expectations, build team connections, and establish performance baseline.

**Owner:** HR Lead (coordination), Functional Manager (role training), Buddy (peer support)

**Onboarding Schedule:**

**Day 1:**
- 9:00 AM: Arrive, IT setup verification with buddy
- 9:30 AM: Welcome meeting with HR Lead (15 min) - company overview, policies, handbook review
- 10:00 AM: Functional Manager 1:1 (30 min) - role clarity, first week plan, team introduction
- 10:30 AM: Office tour + team lunch
- Afternoon: Buddy-led first tasks, documentation completion

**Week 1 (Days 1-5):**
- Daily: Check-in with buddy (10 min)
- Product training (2-3 hours): Company offerings, market positioning, client examples
- Platform training (2 hours): Tools used (Asana, Slack, Google Workspace)
- Role-specific training (4-6 hours): Function-specific workflows, expectations, key contacts
- Deliverable: Complete 2-3 small tasks under buddy/manager guidance

**Week 2 (Days 6-10):**
- Daily: 30-min check-in with manager
- Continuation of training modules
- Assign first independent task (guided)
- Meet with 3-4 key cross-functional contacts
- Deliverable: Understand team roadmap, complete first task

**Week 3 (Days 11-15):**
- Bi-daily: Check-in with manager
- Take on 2-3 independent tasks
- Participate in team meeting (observe + brief contribution)
- One-on-one coffee/chat with peer outside immediate team
- Deliverable: Contribute to team project, 70% task completion rate

**Week 4 (Days 16-30):**
- Weekly: Check-in with manager
- Full task ownership expected
- Transition from buddy to independent work
- Prepare for Day 30 review meeting
- Deliverable: Demonstrate competency in role-specific deliverables

**30-Day Milestones & Checkpoints:**

| Checkpoint | Date | Criteria | Owner | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|-------|
| Day 3 Review | Day 3 | IT setup complete, onboarding on track | Manager | Informal |
| Week 2 Check-in | Day 10 | Training progressing, no blockers, team connection | Manager | Formal meeting |
| Week 3 Review | Day 15 | Independent task completion, cultural fit observed | Manager | 20-min sync |
| Day 30 Review | Day 30 | **Formal Onboarding Review** | Manager + HR | See below |

**Day 30 Formal Onboarding Review:**
- Duration: 30 minutes
- Attendees: Employee, Manager, HR Lead (optional)
- Agenda:
  1. Self-assessment: What went well? Challenges? Feedback?
  2. Manager feedback: Performance vs. expectations, strengths, development areas
  3. Decision: Continue probation, extend onboarding, or escalate concerns
  4. Next steps: Month 2-3 expectations, development plan
- Outcome: Document in personnel file, set next review date (Day 60 if needed)

**Onboarding Documentation:**
- [ ] Signed policy acknowledgment forms
- [ ] Completed emergency contact form
- [ ] Completed bank details form (for payroll)
- [ ] Insurance beneficiary forms (if applicable)
- [ ] Day 30 review notes

**Tools Used:** Google Sheets (onboarding tracker), Asana (task assignments), Google Docs (training materials)

### 2.3 Status Changes

**Purpose:** Manage career progression and role transitions while ensuring compliance and fairness.

#### 2.3.1 Promotions

**Promotion Criteria:**
- **Performance:** Rating 4+ in last 2 reviews
- **Tenure:** Minimum 12 months at current level
- **Manager Recommendation:** Explicit support from current manager
- **CEO Approval:** Final decision authority
- **Market alignment:** Promoted role has budget allocation

**Promotion Timeline:**

| Stage | Owner | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 1. Manager identifies candidate | Manager | Ongoing |
| 2. Manager → HR discuss readiness | Manager + HR Lead | 1-2 weeks |
| 3. HR prepares promotion brief (role, new salary, effective date) | HR Lead | 3-5 days |
| 4. CEO review and approval | CEO | 3-5 days |
| 5. HR communicates offer | HR Lead | 1 day |
| 6. Employee accepts | Employee | 1-2 days |
| 7. Announce to team | Manager | Next team meeting |
| 8. Update systems (payroll, title, reports) | HR Lead + Finance | Effective date |

**Promotion Approval Criteria (CEO Checklist):**
- [ ] Performance justifies promotion
- [ ] Role is budgeted
- [ ] New salary is within market range
- [ ] Manager supports transition
- [ ] No pending disciplinary actions

**Promotion Package:**
- New title and role description
- New base salary (typically +15-25%)
- New reporting line (if applicable)
- Updated responsibilities document
- Effective date (end of month preferred)

**Communication Template:**
```

effective [Date].

New role: [Brief description]
New base salary: ₹[Amount]/month (increase of [%])

outstanding performance and potential. We look forward to your continued
contributions at this level.

Please confirm your acceptance by [Date].

Best regards,
[CEO/HR Lead]
```

**Documentation:** Signed promotion letter in personnel file

#### 2.3.2 Transfers Between Teams

**Trigger:** Business need, employee request, performance improvement

**Transfer Process:**

| Step | Owner | Timeline |
|------|-------|----------|
| 1. Current manager informed | HR Lead or CEO | 1 day |
| 2. New manager brief and agreement | HR Lead | 1-2 days |
| 3. Employee discussion and consent | New Manager | 1-2 days |
| 4. Documentation: transfer letter, updated responsibilities | HR Lead | 1-2 days |
| 5. Announcement and handover plan | Both Managers | 1 day |
| 6. Systems update (Slack, Drive access, reporting) | HR Lead | Effective date |

**Handover Checklist:**
- [ ] Knowledge transfer meetings (3-5 days)
- [ ] Document transition of responsibilities
- [ ] Team introductions and role clarity
- [ ] Confirm new reporting line
- [ ] Update all systems (org chart, email groups, project access)

**Documentation:** Transfer letter signed by both managers and HR Lead

#### 2.3.3 Role Expansions

**Purpose:** Increase employee scope without formal promotion, test capability, or meet business needs.

**Expansion Request Process:**
- Manager identifies scope increase and communicates to employee
- HR reviews compensation impact (may include additional allowance or future promotion consideration)
- CEO approval for new responsibilities
- Document in role description or expansion letter
- Review effectiveness in 60 days

**SLA:** Decision within 1 week of manager proposal

### 2.4 Offboarding

**Purpose:** Ensure smooth transition, knowledge retention, compliance with labor law, and positive alumni relationship.

**Trigger:** Resignation letter, termination notice, contract end

**Owner:** HR Lead + Functional Manager

**Offboarding Timeline:**

**Notice Period:**
- Senior Leads: 30 days
- Interns: 15 days
- Probationary Period: Mutual 7 days
- If terminated for cause: Immediate or 7 days per policy

**Offboarding Process (during notice period):**

**Week 1 of Notice:**
- [ ] Schedule exit interview (after final week)
- [ ] Create knowledge transfer plan with manager
- [ ] Identify documents/assets to be returned
- [ ] Brief on final settlement computation
- [ ] Email: "Notice of Resignation Received" with process overview

**Week 2-3 of Notice:**
- [ ] Employee conducts knowledge transfer sessions (4+ hours per peer)
- [ ] Document handover: Create wiki page or document with processes

- [ ] Manager feedback: Final performance notes for record
- [ ] HR: Verify final leave balance and encashment eligibility

**Final Week:**
- [ ] Exit interview (30-45 min) - see template below
- [ ] Final asset inventory: Laptop, phone, access cards, documents
- [ ] Settlement computation review with employee
- [ ] System deprovisioning: Email forwarding setup, Slack suspension, remove from groups
- [ ] Last day: Collect signed settlement letter, conduct IT handover

- [ ] Email archives downloaded (if required)

**Final Settlement Computation:**

| Component | Calculation | Notes |

| **Less: Tax on Termination Gratuity** | Applicable TDS | Calculate and deduct as per IT Act |
| **Less: Loans/Advances** | Any outstanding employee debt | Clear before final settlement |
| **Total Settlement Amount** | Sum of above | Paid within 2-7 days of last day |

## 3. Performance Management

### 3.1 Goal Setting (OKR Framework)

**Purpose:** Align individual efforts with company objectives, drive accountability, and enable growth.

**OKR Framework Hierarchy:**

```
Company OKRs (CEO defines)
├─ Team OKRs (Leads define)
└─ Individual OKRs (Employees define with manager)
```

**OKR Cycle:** Quarterly (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4) with annual review

**OKR Definition:**
- **Objective:** Qualitative direction (what we want to achieve)
- **Key Results:** 3-4 measurable outcomes (how we know we succeeded)
- **Target:** 70% achievement = successful (ambitious but realistic)

**Quarterly OKR Process:**

| Stage | Timeline | Owner | Deliverable |
|-------|----------|-------|-------------|
| 1. CEO defines Company OKRs | 1 week before quarter start | CEO | 3-5 company OKRs |
| 2. Leads define Team OKRs | 1 week after company OKRs | Leads + CEO input | 2-3 team OKRs per team |
| 3. Leads share with teams | 2 days after | Leads | Team kickoff meeting |
| 4. Employees draft Individual OKRs | 3-4 days after | Employees + Manager input | Individual OKR draft |
| 5. Manager reviews and provides feedback | 2-3 days after | Managers | Approved individual OKRs |
| 6. HR consolidates and publishes | 1 day before quarter | HR Lead | OKR tracker published |

**OKR Template:**

```
INDIVIDUAL OKR - [Name] - [Quarter]

Objective 1: [Qualitative goal aligned to team OKR]
  ├─ Key Result 1: [Metric] from [Baseline] to [Target]
  ├─ Key Result 2: [Metric] from [Baseline] to [Target]
  └─ Key Result 3: [Metric] from [Baseline] to [Target]

Objective 2: [Qualitative goal]
  ├─ Key Result 1: [Metric]
  └─ Key Result 2: [Metric]

Supporting OKR (if applicable): [Lower priority goal]

Alignment: Links to [Team OKR], [Company OKR]
Owner: [Employee Name]
Manager: [Manager Name]
Status: [On Track / At Risk / Off Track] (updated monthly)
```

**OKR Monitoring:**
- **Monthly:** Employee updates status (On Track/At Risk/Off Track) with brief commentary
- **1:1s:** Review progress, address blockers, adjust if needed
- **End of Quarter:** Final scoring (% achievement, 0-100%)

**OKR Scoring Rubric:**
- **100%:** Exceeded all KRs
- **70-99%:** Achieved most KRs; 70% is the target success level
- **40-69%:** Achieved some KRs; identify learning and next-quarter adjustments
- **<40%:** Missed majority of KRs; investigate blockers and support needed

**Tools Used:** Google Sheets (OKR tracker), Asana (task alignment)

### 3.2 Continuous Feedback

**Purpose:** Provide real-time feedback, unblock progress, and build psychological safety for growth.

**1:1 Cadence:**

| Level | Frequency | Duration | Platform |
|-------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Interns | Weekly | 30 min | Video (Zoom/Meet) + in-person |
| Junior (Post-intern) | Bi-weekly | 30 min | Video |
| Mid-level | Bi-weekly | 45 min | Video |
| Senior/Leads | Monthly (plus ad-hoc) | 45 min | Video or in-person |

**1:1 Agenda Framework (30-45 min):**

1. **Wins & Celebrations (5 min):**
   - What went well this week?
   - Any wins to celebrate?

2. **Challenges & Blockers (10 min):**
   - What's slowing you down?
   - Do you need support?
   - Any roadblocks preventing progress?

3. **OKR & Metrics Review (10 min):**
   - Progress on current OKRs (% completion)
   - Key metrics or deliverables on track?
   - Any adjustments needed?

4. **Development & Learning (10 min):**
   - What skills do you want to develop?
   - Any training or learning opportunities?
   - Feedback on recent work/project

5. **Action Items & Next Steps (5 min):**
   - Clear action items (owner, due date)
   - Next 1:1 agenda topics
   - Confirm date of next meeting

**1:1 Documentation:**
- Manager records brief notes in shared doc or platform
- Share key action items with employee (via email or chat)
- Save 1:1 notes in [Employee] folder in Google Drive
- Format: Date | Wins | Challenges | OKR Status | Actions | Next Meeting Date

**1:1 Example Note:**
```
1:1 Notes - [Employee Name] - [Date]

Wins:
- Completed API integration ahead of schedule
- Helped onboard new intern

Challenges:
- Waiting on design feedback from stakeholder
- Need clarity on Q2 project scope

OKR Status:
- KR1 (Complete 2 projects): 1/2 done, on track
- KR2 (Improve response time): 8 hours avg, target is 6 hours

Development:
- Interested in learning React; suggest online course

Actions:
- [Employee]: Follow up with design team on feedback (by Thursday)
- [Manager]: Clarify Q2 project scope (by Monday)

Next Meeting: [Date] at [Time]
```

**Feedback Framework (SBI Model):**
- **Situation:** Specific context ("In the client call yesterday...")
- **Behavior:** Observable action ("...you interrupted Sarah twice...")
- **Impact:** Effect on others/work ("...which made her feel unheard and hurt collaboration")

**Giving Feedback Conversation:**
1. Schedule 1:1 when calm
2. Use SBI: "In [situation], I noticed [behavior]. The impact was [effect]."
3. Ask: "What was going on from your perspective?"
4. Listen actively, don't defend
5. Collaborate: "How can we approach this differently?"
6. Confirm understanding and action

**Receiving Feedback (Employee Responsibility):**
- Listen without interrupting
- Clarify: "So you observed...? Help me understand the impact."
- Thank the manager for feedback
- Reflect and follow up on actions

**Documentation Platform Options:**
- Google Docs (shared 1:1 folder per employee)
- Asana (tasks + comments)
- Notion (team wiki with 1:1 section)
- HR platform (if scaled later)

**Tools Used:** Google Drive, Asana, Zoom

### 3.3 Performance Reviews

**Purpose:** Formally assess performance, provide development feedback, and inform compensation decisions.

**Review Cadence:**
- **Quarterly Mini-Reviews:** 30 min, lightweight, development focused
- **Annual Comprehensive Review:** 60 min, formal assessment, compensation impact

#### 3.3.1 Quarterly Mini-Review

**Timing:** End of Q1, Q2, Q3 (Q4 rolls into Annual)

**Duration:** 30 minutes (Manager + Employee)

**Agenda:**
1. OKR achievement summary (% complete)
2. Key accomplishments this quarter
3. Strengths demonstrated
4. One development area for next quarter
5. Calibrated rating (context only, not formal record)
6. Next quarter focus and any support needed

**Format:** Conversation-based, focus on development not evaluation

**Documentation:** Brief notes in personnel file, no formal rating recorded

#### 3.3.2 Annual Comprehensive Review

**Timing:** Last 2 weeks of February (effective March 1; aligns with compensation review)

**Duration:** 60 minutes minimum

**Participants:** Manager, Employee, HR Lead (optional, only if policy issue)

**Pre-Review Self-Assessment (Employee prepares):**

```

   - [Project/achievement 2]

   - Q1 Average Score: ___%
   - Q2 Average Score: ___%
   - Q3 Average Score: ___%
   - Annual Average: ___%

   - [Skill 1]: Proficiency level
   - [Skill 2]: Proficiency level
   - [Skill 3]: Proficiency level

4. Development Areas:
   - Area 1: How I'm improving
   - Area 2: How I'm improving

   - Certifications/training completed

   - Team contributions

### 4.4 Annual Performance Bonus

**Purpose:** Recognize annual performance and align with profitability.

**Bonus Eligibility:**
- Employed for full fiscal year (or prorated if joined during year)
- Not on PIP at year-end
- Performance rating 3+ (Meets Expectations or higher)
- No active disciplinary actions

**Bonus Calculation:**

```
Annual Bonus = Base Salary × Bonus % × Performance Multiplier

Example 1: Junior at ₹30K/month, Rating 4
  Annual Bonus = ₹30K × 12 months × 5% base × 1.25 multiplier (Rating 4)
               = ₹360K × 0.05 × 1.25
               = ₹22,500

Example 2: Senior at ₹1,50K/month, Rating 3
  Annual Bonus = ₹1,50K × 12 months × 8% base × 1.0 multiplier (Rating 3)
               = ₹1,800K × 0.08 × 1.0
               = ₹144,000
```

**Bonus by Performance Rating:**

| Rating | Bonus Multiplier | Bonus % of Salary |
|--------|---|---|
| 5 (Exceptional) | 1.5x | 12-15% |
| 4 (Exceeds) | 1.25x | 10-12% |
| 3 (Meets) | 1.0x | 8-10% |
| 2 (Below) | 0.5x | 2-4% |
| 1 (Significantly Below) | 0x | Ineligible |

**Bonus Payment Timing:**
- Announced in March/April (post-performance review)
- Paid in April salary or separate check
- Tax deducted as applicable (TDS)

**Bonus Documentation:**

## 5. Leave & Benefits

### 5.1 Leave Types & Accrual

**Leave Policy Applicable Law:** Industrial Disputes Act, 1947; State Shops & Establishment Acts

#### 5.1.1 Earned Leave (EL)

**Accrual:** 18 days per calendar year (1.5 days per month)

**Rules:**
- Accrues monthly on the last day of month
- Can carry forward max 30 days to next year
- Remaining balance beyond 30 days forfeited (or encashable, per policy)
- Encashable at exit for all eligible employees
- Encashment rate: (Basic Salary / 26) × Days encashed

**Approval:** Manager approval within 24 hours of request

**Usage:** Can be taken in full days or half days; max 3 consecutive days without medical cert

#### 5.1.2 Sick Leave (SL)

**Accrual:** 12 days per calendar year

**Rules:**
- Non-encashable, non-carryable
- Used for genuine illness or medical appointments
- Medical certificate required for >2 consecutive days
- Cannot plan sick leave; must be approved when intimated
- Manager may request proof of illness

**Approval:** Same-day approval by manager for immediate notification

**Usage:** Full or half days; documented in system

#### 5.1.3 Casual Leave (CL)

**Accrual:** 12 days per calendar year

**Rules:**
- Non-encashable, non-carryable (forfeited at year-end)
- Used for personal work, family matters
- Must request in advance (min 1 day notice)
- Max 3 consecutive days per instance
- Not approved if no annual balance

**Approval:** Manager approval at least 1 day in advance

**Usage:** Full or half days

#### 5.1.4 Maternity Leave

**Eligibility:** All female employees after 6 months of employment

**Duration:** 26 weeks (6 months) as per Maternity Benefit Act, 2017
- 8 weeks before expected delivery
- 18 weeks after delivery (including miscarriage cases)

**Additional Benefits:**
- Full salary during maternity leave (non-deductible from CTC)
- Medical reimbursement (hospitalization, delivery costs)
- Choice to extend unpaid leave (up to 6 months additional)
- Creche facility (if scaled to 50+ employees)
- Lactation breaks: 2 breaks of 30 min each for 6 months post-delivery

**Process:**
1. Employee notifies HR on confirmation of pregnancy
2. Medical certificate provided with expected delivery date
3. HR calculates eligible leave duration
4. Employee confirms dates before delivery
5. Post-delivery: Employee provides hospital discharge summary
6. Payroll updated, leave adjusted if delivery is early/late

**Documentation:** Medical certificates and hospital records stored

#### 5.1.5 Paternity Leave

**Eligibility:** All male employees

**Duration:** 5 days (paid)

**Usage:** Must be taken within 3 months of child's birth

**Approval:** Manager approval with proof of child's birth (hospital certificate or birth registration)

#### 5.1.6 Public Holidays

**National Holidays (10 days):**
- Republic Day (Jan 26)
- Holi (March, date varies)
- Good Friday (March, date varies)
- Eid-ul-Fitr (Date varies)
- Eid-ul-Adha (Date varies)
- Independence Day (Aug 15)
- Janmashtami (Date varies)
- Gandhi Jayanti (Oct 2)
- Diwali (Oct/Nov, date varies)
- Christmas (Dec 25)

**State Holidays (Telangana - 2 restricted holidays):**
- Telangana Formation Day (June 2)
- Ayyodhya Day (Nov 6) or other state-specific holiday

**Holiday Rules:**
- Paid holidays, no deduction from leave balance
- If public holiday falls on weekend, holiday is not carried to next working day
- Employees working on public holiday get compensatory off or special pay

#### 5.1.7 Compensatory Off (CO)

**Purpose:** Compensation for work done on weekends or public holidays

**Rules:**
- CO must be taken within 30 days of the work date
- Cannot be encashed (except at exit if allowed by policy)
- Manager must approve CO request in advance
- Max 1 CO per weekend worked
- CO does not impact leave balance

**Process:**
1. Manager identifies need for weekend/holiday work
2. Employee notified of CO benefit
3. Employee requests CO within 30 days, manager approves
4. Marked in leave system as CO taken

### 5.3 Leave Balance & Carry-Forward Rules

**Annual Carry-Forward (Jan 1):**

| Leave Type | Carry-Forward Limit | Forfeiture |
|---|---|---|
| Earned Leave | 30 days max | Balance >30 forfeited (or encashed at exit) |
| Sick Leave | 0 days (non-carryable) | All unused SL forfeited |
| Casual Leave | 0 days (non-carryable) | All unused CL forfeited |

**Leave Encashment:**
- At exit: EL encashed at (Basic/26) × Days
- During service: Per policy decision (not automatic)
- Limit: Cannot encash >30 days in a year
- Tax: TDS deducted, reported on Form 16

**Example Carry-Forward Calculation (Dec 31):**
```
Earned Leave Usage:
  Granted in 2025:        18 days
  Used in 2025:          -10 days

#### 5.4.4 Gratuity (Severance Benefit)

**Applicable Law:** Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972

**Eligibility:**
- After 5 years of continuous employment
- Applicable to all employees (interns if employed >5 years)
- Payable at retirement, resignation, termination (except for cause)

**Gratuity Calculation:**

```
Gratuity = (Basic Salary × Years of Service × 15) / 26

Example: Employee with 10 years service, Basic ₹50,000/month
  Gratuity = (₹50,000 × 10 × 15) / 26
           = ₹7,500,000 / 26
           = ₹2,88,462 (approximately)
```

**Gratuity Accrual:**
- Accrues monthly (employee earns benefit from day 1)
- Provision made in financial statements
- Paid in full at separation after 5 years

**Gratuity Non-Forfeiture:**
- If employee leaves before 5 years: No gratuity (unless termination without cause)
- If terminated for cause: No gratuity
- If terminated without cause: Gratuity payable (even <5 years, at company discretion)
- If terminated on grounds of misconduct/theft/violence: No gratuity

**Gratuity Tax Treatment:**
- Tax exemption: Up to ₹10 lakhs (per Income Tax Act)
- Amount over ₹10L: Taxable as income in year of receipt
- TDS: Deducted if gratuity >₹10L
- No TDS if <₹10L (still included in annual income tax filing)

**Gratuity Computation & Payment:**
- Computed at separation
- Included in final settlement
- Paid within 7 days of separation (or per employee's choice)
- Documented in settlement letter

**Gratuity Provision (Accounting):**
- Annually computed (March 31)
- Included in balance sheet as liability
- Updated during compensation review
- Example: 16 employees with varying tenures = estimated gratuity liability

### 6.1 Grievance Handling

**Purpose:** Provide fair, transparent process for addressing employee concerns and workplace issues.

**Applicable Law:** Industrial Disputes Act, 1947; Labor Code, 2020 (Dispute Resolution)

**Grievance Definition:** Any concern, complaint, or dissatisfaction related to employment (compensation, treatment, benefits, working conditions, etc.)

**Grievance Process:**

```
Employee Files Grievance (written, to HR)
    ↓
HR Acknowledges (within 24 hours)
    ↓
Investigation (7 days)
    │
    ├─ Gather facts and evidence
    ├─ Interview relevant parties
    └─ Document findings
    ↓
Resolution (within 15 days of filing)
    │
    ├─ Provide resolution
    ├─ Communicate decision to employee
    └─ Document outcome
    ↓
Appeal Period (if employee dissatisfied)
    │
    └─ CEO review (if requested)
    ↓
Closure & Documentation
```

**Grievance Filing:**

**Grievance Form:**
```
EMPLOYEE GRIEVANCE FORM

Employee Name: ____  Employee ID: ____  Date Filed: ____
Manager: ____  Department: ____

Grievance Details:
Type of Issue: [ ] Compensation [ ] Work Environment [ ] Behavior/Conduct
               [ ] Discrimination [ ] Safety [ ] Benefits [ ] Other: ____

Description of Issue:
[Detailed description, dates, impacts, previous attempts to resolve]

Resolution Sought:
[What resolution would satisfy the employee]

Attached Evidence: [ ] Yes [ ] No
  If yes: [List documents, emails, etc.]

Employee Signature: ____  Date: ____

FOR HR USE ONLY:
Received by: [HR Lead Name]  Date/Time: ____
Acknowledgment Sent: [Date/Time]
```

**Grievance Filing Options:**
1. **Formal:** Submitted in writing to HR (email or form)
2. **Informal:** Discussed verbally with manager first (optional, but encouraged)
3. **Anonymous:** Through HR hotline (if scale permits, future feature)

**HR Acknowledgment (within 24 hours):**
```
Subject: Grievance Acknowledgment - [Ref Number]

Dear [Employee Name],

Thank you for submitting your grievance on [Date]. We have received and
registered your complaint with reference number [GRV-001].

We will investigate and provide resolution within 15 days.

HR Lead
```

**Investigation Phase (Days 1-7):**

| Step | Action | Owner | Timeline |
|------|--------|-------|----------|
| 1. Document Review | Collect relevant emails, documents, evidence | HR Lead | 1-2 days |
| 2. Stakeholder Interviews | Interview employee, involved parties, witnesses | HR Lead | 3-4 days |
| 3. Fact Finding | Analyze and document facts | HR Lead | 1-2 days |
| 4. Manager Input | Manager provides perspective (if not party) | Manager | 2 days |
| 5. Evidence Compilation | Organize findings in investigation report | HR Lead | 1 day |

**Investigation Report Template:**
```
GRIEVANCE INVESTIGATION REPORT

2. [Fact 2 with evidence]
3. [Fact 3 with evidence]

- Employee: [Date, key points]

[Grievance founded or unfounded]

RECOMMENDATION:
[Suggested resolution]

Submitted by: [HR Lead]  Date: ____
```

**Resolution Phase (Days 8-15):**

**Resolution Types:**

1. **Grievance Founded (Policy Violated):**
   - Issue raised with responsible party
   - Corrective action taken (warning, policy adjustment, compensation, etc.)
   - Employee notified of resolution
   - Monitor for recurrence

2. **Grievance Partially Founded:**
   - Some aspects validated
   - Partial resolution offered
   - Clear expectations set for future
   - Employee informed

3. **Grievance Not Founded:**
   - Facts do not support grievance
   - Clear explanation provided to employee
   - Appeal process outlined
   - Closed unless appealed

**Resolution Communication:**

```
Subject: Grievance Resolution - [Ref Number]

Dear [Employee Name],

We have completed our investigation into your grievance filed on [Date].
Based on our findings:

[SUMMARY OF FINDINGS]
[RESOLUTION OR DECISION]
[ACTIONS TAKEN]
[EXPECTED IMPROVEMENTS]

request to the CEO.

HR Lead
Date: ____
```

**Appeal Process (if employee disputes resolution):**
- Employee submits appeal to CEO within 7 days
- CEO reviews investigation and resolution within 5 days
- CEO's decision is final
- Communicate outcome within 3 days

**Grievance Documentation:**
- Filed form + investigation report + resolution decision
- Stored in confidential grievance file (separate from personnel file)
- Access: HR Lead, CEO only
- Retention: 3 years after resolution

**Grievance Metrics (for HR reporting):**
- Total grievances filed per month
- Resolution time average
- Grievance types breakdown
- Repeat issues identification
- Close rate

### 6.2 Workplace Conduct & Disciplinary Action

**Purpose:** Maintain professional workplace environment and address policy violations fairly.

**Applicable Law:** Industrial Disputes Act; Shops & Establishment Act

**Code of Conduct (Core Expectations):**
- Professional and respectful behavior with colleagues, managers, clients
- Honesty and integrity in all work
- Compliance with laws and company policies
- Confidentiality of company and client information
- No discrimination or harassment
- Safety compliance
- No substance abuse
- Timely work delivery

**Conduct Violations (Examples):**

**Minor (First Warning):**
- Repeated lateness without excuse
- Incomplete work submission
- Missed meetings without notice
- Minor policy violation

**Moderate (Suspension Risk):**
- Insubordination (refusing manager instruction)
- Conflict with colleagues or client
- Confidentiality breach (minor)
- Absence without approval (1-2 days)

**Serious (Termination Risk):**
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or harassment
- Major confidentiality breach (data theft)
- Working for competitor
- Prolonged absence without approval
- Intoxication at work
- Falsification of documents

**Disciplinary Action Process:**

```
Conduct Violation Observed/Reported
    ↓
Manager Documents Incident
    ↓
HR Lead Reviews (investigation needed?)
    ├─ Minor: Verbal warning + documentation
    └─ Moderate/Serious: Formal investigation
    ↓
Formal Investigation (if serious)
    │
    ├─ Gather evidence
    ├─ Interview parties
    └─ Draft investigation report
    ↓
Disciplinary Hearing
    │
    ├─ Present findings to employee
    ├─ Employee given opportunity to respond
    └─ Decision communicated
    ↓
Disciplinary Action
    │
    ├─ [ ] Verbal Warning
    ├─ [ ] Written Warning
    ├─ [ ] Suspension
    └─ [ ] Termination
    ↓
Documentation & Appeals
    │
    └─ Right to appeal to CEO (7 days)
    ↓
Closure
```

**Disciplinary Action Levels:**

**Level 1: Verbal Warning**
- For minor, first-time violations
- Manager discusses with employee
- Documented: Date, issue, expectation, manager, witness
- No salary impact
- Cleared after 3 months of good behavior

**Level 2: Written Warning**
- For repeated minor violations or moderate first violation
- Formal letter issued
- Meeting to communicate and discuss expectations
- Document: Issue, policy violated, expectation, improvement timeline, consequence of non-compliance
- Placed in personnel file
- 6-month improvement period; cleared if no further issues

**Level 3: Suspension**
- For serious violations (insubordination, harassment, theft, etc.)
- Duration: 3-7 days (without pay)
- Formal letter with suspension notice
- Investigation may precede or follow suspension
- Condition to return: Written commitment to policy compliance
- Documented in personnel file
- Future violation may lead to termination

**Level 4: Termination**
- For gross misconduct (fraud, violence, major breach of trust)
- For PIP failure and consistent non-performance
- Formal termination notice
- Payment of final settlement (less any damages owed)
- Reference: May not be positive
- Follow offboarding process

**Disciplinary Warning Letter Template:**

```
DISCIPLINARY WARNING NOTICE

Employee Name: ____  ID: ____  Date: ____
Manager: ____  Department: ____
Warning Level: [ ] Verbal [ ] Written [ ] Suspension [ ] Termination

[Clear description of violation with dates and facts]

POLICY/RULE VIOLATED:
[Cite specific policy]

IMPACT:
[How this affected work, team, company]

EXPECTED IMPROVEMENT:
[Clear expectation for future conduct]

CONSEQUENCE OF NON-COMPLIANCE:
[Next disciplinary step if issue repeats]

IMPROVEMENT PERIOD: __ days/months

Employee Acknowledgment:
I have reviewed this warning and understand the expectations.

Employee Signature: ____  Date: ____
Manager Signature: ____  Date: ____
Witness (HR): ____  Date: ____

For HR File Only: [ ] Stored in Personnel File
```

**Disciplinary Hearing Protocol:**
1. **Notification:** Employee given 48 hours notice of hearing
2. **Right to Respond:** Employee may present their side
3. **Representation:** Employee may bring colleague as support (not legal counsel, typically)
4. **Evidence:** Review documents and witness statements
5. **Decision:** Communicate outcome in writing within 3 days
6. **Appeal:** Employee has 7 days to appeal to CEO

**Termination for Cause (Summary Dismissal):**
- Used for gross misconduct only (theft, violence, major policy breach)
- No notice period; immediate separation
- Final settlement: Salary to date, forfeiture of notice period payment
- Gratuity forfeited
- Negative reference

**Termination without Cause:**
- 30 days notice (or payment in lieu)
- Salary throughout notice period
- Gratuity paid (after 5 years)
- Leave encashment paid
- Neutral reference

### 6.3 Anti-Harassment & POSH Compliance

**Applicable Law:** Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition & Redressal) Act, 2013 (POSH Act)

**InTime POSH Compliance Status:**
- Current headcount: 16 (below 10-employee threshold)
- **Trigger:** When headcount reaches 10+, establish Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)
- **Current:** HR Lead serves as informal point of contact for complaints
- **Q1 2026:** Plan to establish formal ICC if headcount grows

**Anti-Harassment Policy (applies to all employees):**

**Zero Tolerance for:**
- Sexual harassment (unwelcome sexual advances, requests, conduct)
- Gender-based harassment
- Casteism
- Discrimination based on religion, caste, gender, disability
- Bullying, intimidation, or verbal abuse
- Threats or retaliation

**Reporting Mechanisms:**

**Current (Headcount <10):**
- **Informal:** Discuss with manager (if manager is not involved)
- **Formal:** Write to HR Lead (confidential)
- **Escalation:** Report directly to CEO (if HR Lead involved)

**Future (Headcount 10+):**
- Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)
- **Members:**
  - Senior woman employee (chair)
  - 1 HR/management representative
  - 1 external expert (counselor or legal)
  - 1 employee representative
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Receive and investigate complaints
  - Ensure confidentiality
  - Recommend action to CEO
  - Maintain records

**Complaint Process:**

```
Employee Files Complaint (written, confidential)
    ↓
HR/ICC Acknowledges (within 24 hours)
    ↓
Investigation (within 30 days)
    │
    ├─ Interview complainant
    ├─ Interview respondent
    ├─ Interview witnesses
    └─ Gather evidence
    ↓
Report & Recommendation
    │
    └─ CEO/ICC decides action
    ↓
Action (warning, suspension, termination, if founded)
    ↓
Complainant & Respondent Notified (separately, confidentially)
    ↓
Appeal Process (if respondent disputes)
    ↓
Closure & Follow-up
```

**Investigation Standards:**
- Confidentiality: Names and details protected
- Impartiality: Investigation by unbiased party
- No retaliation: Complainant protected from reprisals
- No victimization of witnesses
- Timely resolution (30-60 days)

**Outcomes & Actions:**
- **Founded:** Disciplinary action (warning, suspension, termination) against respondent
- **Not Founded:** Closure with explanation
- **Partially Founded:** Partial disciplinary action

**Confidentiality & Records:**
- Complaint file kept separate from personnel file
- Access restricted to HR/ICC/CEO only
- Records retained for 3 years
- Case details not disclosed beyond need-to-know

**Prevention & Awareness:**
- Annual training for all employees (when ICC established)
- Policy communication at onboarding
- Posters and notices in office
- Regular reminders in team meetings

## 9. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What is the notice period?**
A: 30 days for Senior Leads, 15 days for interns. Probation period: 7 days mutual.

**Q: Can I carry forward unused casual leave?**
A: No. Casual leave is non-carryable and forfeited at year-end.

**Q: When do I get my first salary review/increase?**
A: Annual review in March for all employees. First year employees reviewed based on probation completion and performance.

**Q: How are OKRs different from KPIs?**
A: OKRs are ambitious goals for the quarter (70% achievement = success). KPIs are ongoing metrics (target = 100%).

**Q: Can I request a leave transfer if I don't use all my earned leave?**
A: You can carry forward max 30 days to the next year. Amount beyond 30 days may be encashed (subject to policy).

**Q: What happens if I fail my PIP?**
A: The company will discuss role changes, demotion, or termination based on severity.

**Q: How much gratuity will I receive?**
A: (Basic Salary × Years of Service × 15) / 26, payable after 5 years of continuous employment.

**Q: What if I resign during my notice period?**
A: You must complete the full notice period. Early exit may result in forfeiture of notice period benefits.

**Q: Who should I contact for HR issues?**
A: Contact [HR Lead Name] via email: [email]. For confidential matters, you may escalate to the CEO.
