# Account Acquisition SOP

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** February 2024
**Owner:** Business Development
**Audience:** Business Development Managers (BDMs), Founder, Sales Leadership

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the systematic process for identifying, qualifying, and closing new IT staffing client accounts. This procedure ensures consistent deal flow, professional sales execution, and scalable revenue growth for InTime.

**Scope:** All new account acquisition activities, from prospecting through contract signature and initial onboarding. Applies to US headquarters and India operations expansion.

**Key Success Metrics:**
- Sales Pipeline Value: $500K+ qualified deals in progress
- Conversion Rate: 25%+ of qualified prospects close within 90 days
- Average Deal Size: $150K+ annual contract value (ACV)
- Sales Cycle Length: 30-60 days from first call to MSA signature
- Activity Metrics: 50+ new prospecting touches per week per BDM

---

## 2. IDEAL CLIENT PROFILE (ICP)

Target companies matching these criteria:

**Company Size & Structure:**
- Revenue: $50M - $500M (mid-market focus)
- Employees: 200 - 2,000 total headcount
- IT/Engineering Team: 20+ technical staff (demonstrates hiring need)
- Growth Rate: 15%+ YoY growth (indicates hiring velocity)
- Structure: Decentralized hiring (multiple departments, not single HR gatekeeper)

**Industry Vertical:**
- Technology: SaaS, fintech, cloud, cybersecurity, data analytics
- Financial Services: Banks, credit unions, wealth management, insurance
- Healthcare Tech: Healthcare IT, medical devices, health informatics
- E-commerce & Logistics: Digital platforms, fulfillment, supply chain
- Manufacturing Tech: IoT, industrial automation, MES systems
- Telecom & Media: Streaming, infrastructure, software platforms

**Hiring & Staffing Indicators:**
- Actively hiring: Posted 10+ job openings in past 90 days
- Contract/Flexible Model: History of using contractors or temp labor (indicates openness to staffing)
- Geographic Distribution: Multiple office locations (indicates need for local hiring expertise)
- High Turnover Industry: Tech, media, financial services (predictable recurring need)
- Seasonal Hiring: Projects with known cycle (e.g., retail tech for holiday season)

**Compliance & Risk Profile:**
- No major lawsuits or regulatory issues (check SEC filings)
- Customer Financial Health: Sufficient cash flow (low bankruptcy risk)
- Stability: 3+ years in operation, stable leadership
- Not in Decline: Growing or stable revenue (not on decline trajectory)

**Low-Priority Targets (Skip or Lower Intensity):**
- Government contractors (complex compliance, slow decision-making)
- Non-tech startups <$10M (minimal hiring budget)
- Single-location small businesses <50 employees
- Companies actively down-sizing
- Unprofitable burn-rate startups

|---|---|---|---|

| **Cold Email** | Large volume, decision-makers, VP-level | 3-8% | 2-5 days |
| **LinkedIn InMail** | Premium outreach, senior decision-makers | 10-15% | 1-2 days |
| **Phone (Warm Transfer)** | Referral-based, warm introductions | 40%+ | Same day |
| **Event/Networking** | Relationship-first, high-touch, long cycle | 20%+ | Ongoing |
| **Vendor Portal** | Pre-registered companies, systematic entry | 5-10% | 1-2 weeks |

### PHASE 3: DISCOVERY CALL AND QUALIFICATION

**Step 3.1: Schedule Discovery Call**

Once prospect agrees to call (via email, LinkedIn, or phone):

Email confirmation:

```
Perfect. I've sent you a calendar invite for [Day, Time, Timezone].

I'll call you at [Your Phone #] at [Time]. The call will be about 20-30 minutes.
Just to set expectations, I'll be asking some questions about your team's
hiring plans and challenges - this will help me understand if there's a fit.

Looking forward to connecting.
```

**Discovery Call Details:**
- Duration: 25-30 minutes
- Timing: Early in week (Mon-Wed), mid-morning or early afternoon (avoid Friday)
- Format: Phone or Zoom (ask prospect preference)
- Participants: BDM + possibly sales manager/founder (if warm introduction or strategic account)

**Step 3.2: Conduct BANT + Staffing Qualification**

**BANT Framework** (standard B2B sales qualification):

| Criteria | What to Uncover | Sample Questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Budget** | Do they have funding/budget for staffing? | "How does your team budget for contractor/staffing costs?" "What's the typical cost per hire for your open roles?" |
| **Authority** | Is this person a decision-maker or do we need others? | "Are you the one who approves vendor selection, or is there a procurement process?" "Who else should I be talking to about staffing?" |
| **Need** | Do they have actual hiring need (not theoretical)? | "What are your top 3 hiring priorities right now?" "How many open positions do you have?" "What's the timeline?" |
| **Timeline** | When would they hire and by when? | "When are you looking to fill these roles?" "What's your ideal start date?" |

**Additional Staffing-Specific Questions:**

| Topic | Sample Questions |
|---|---|
| **Current Hiring Process** | "Walk me through how you currently hire for [Role Type]. What's working? What's frustrating?" "What's your typical time-to-fill?" |
| **Contractor/Flexible Staffing Use** | "Have you used contract or temp labor before?" "Any experience with staffing agencies?" "What was that experience like?" |
| **Hiring Challenges** | "What's been your biggest challenge filling [Role Type]?" "What gaps do you see in candidates you're interviewing?" |
| **Specific Open Roles** | "Tell me about your open roles. What are you looking to fill?" "How many [Role Type] engineers do you need?" "What's the timeline?" |
| **Volume & Frequency** | "How many engineers do you typically hire per year?" "Is this a one-time need or ongoing?" |
| **Rate/Budget** | "What's your budget for this role?" "Are we talking contract or permanent hire?" |
| **Internal vs. External** | "Do you have internal recruiters, or do you outsource?" "Would you be open to working with an external staffing partner?" |
| **Vendor Management** | "Do you have preferred vendors, or would you add a new partner?" "What's your vendor registration process?" |

**Qualification Decision Matrix:**

| Criteria | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Need** (Active Hiring) | 1-3 | 3 = Actively hiring now; 2 = Planning in 1-3 months; 1 = Exploratory/future |
| **Authority** | 1-3 | 3 = Direct decision-maker; 2 = Recommender + approver; 1 = Influencer only |
| **Budget** | 1-3 | 3 = Budget confirmed, approved; 2 = Budget likely, pending approval; 1 = Budget unclear |
| **Timeline** | 1-3 | 3 = Hire within 30 days; 2 = Hire within 60 days; 1 = Hire 90+ days out or unknown |
| **Openness to Staffing** | 1-3 | 3 = Enthusiastic; 2 = Open; 1 = Skeptical or entrenched with competitor |

**Total Qualification Score:**
- 13-15: **Qualified (SAL)** - Sales Accepted Lead, move to proposal
- 10-12: **Nurture** - Stay in contact; monthly check-in; low priority
- <10: **Unqualified** - Polite exit; save for future; minimal follow-up

**Step 3.3: Closing the Discovery Call**

If qualified (Score 13+):

```
"Thanks so much for taking the time to walk me through this. It sounds like
you have [X] open [Role Type] positions, and you're looking to fill by [Date].

Here's what I'm thinking: I'm going to put together a quick overview of how
we work - our fee structure, timeline, bench, that sort of thing. I'll send
that to you by [Day]. Then we can schedule a brief follow-up to discuss next
steps. Does that work?"

[Wait for confirmation]

"Perfect. I'll get that over to you. In the meantime, if you think of any
specific candidates you're already interviewing or any other details about
the role, just shoot me a note. That'll help me make sure we're aligned."
```

If unqualified:

```
"Thanks so much for walking me through this. I really appreciate your time.

It sounds like you're not actively hiring right now, but [specific hiring
trigger], so I imagine staffing will become more relevant then.

Mind if I touch base in [3-6 months] to see how things are progressing?
In the meantime, if anything changes, you know where to find me."
```

### PHASE 5: MSA NEGOTIATION AND VENDOR REGISTRATION

**Step 5.1: Prepare MSA (Master Service Agreement)**

Once prospect is ready to move forward, initiate MSA process:

**Key Terms to Define:**

| Term | InTime Position | Negotiation Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Exclusive vs. Non-Exclusive** | Exclusive preferred | For contract roles: 12-month exclusivity. For permanent: 6-month. Non-exclusive acceptable if rate is higher. |
| **Fee Structure** | Markup model preferred | Contract: 25-35% markup on candidate pay rate OR hourly fee ($X/hour billed to client). Permanent: 20-25% of first-year salary. Negotiate down to 20%/18% if large volume or multi-year commitment. |
| **Payment Terms** | Net 30 preferred | Negotiate to: Net 15 if small company, Net 30 standard, Net 45 acceptable for enterprise. Do not accept Net 60+. |
| **Candidate Guarantee** | 90 days preferred | If candidate leaves or is terminated in 90 days, replace at no charge. Some clients push to 180 days - cap at 120 days max. |
| **Liability Cap** | 1x annual contract value | Standard: neither party liable for indirect damages. Cap liability at total fees paid in preceding 12 months. |
| **Term & Termination** | 2 years preferred | Minimum 1 year, prefer 2 years. Allow termination for cause. Prevent termination without cause in first 90 days. |
| **Rate Increases** | 3-5% annual escalation | If multi-year, include CPI or fixed increase (e.g., 3% Year 2, 3% Year 3). |
| **Non-Solicitation** | Standard clause | Candidate cannot hire away InTime consultant for 12 months after placement. |
| **Non-Compete** | Only if requested | InTime agrees not to compete with [Specific Company Name] for [Specific Roles] in [Geography] for [Term]. Keep narrowly defined. |
| **Vendor Compliance** | Client to determine | Clients may require: background checks on candidates, compliance certifications, insurance requirements, data security agreements. Confirm client requirements early. |

**MSA Negotiation Flow:**

1. **InTime sends draft MSA** (within 2 business days of agreement to move forward)
2. **Client Legal reviews** (expect 3-7 business days for response)
3. **Initial comments** (usually requests for higher guarantees, longer terms, lower fees)
4. **BDM + Founder negotiate** (Founder involved for enterprise accounts or significant disagreements)
5. **Revised MSA** (turn-around within 2 business days)
6. **Final review** (1-2 rounds typical)
7. **Signature & Execution** (both parties sign, agreement is live)

**Fee Negotiation Strategy:**

*Client Push:* "Can you do better on price?"

*InTime Response Option A (Defensive):*
"Our current rate of [X]% reflects the full service we provide - including our India ops bench, interview prep, candidate quality assurance, and replacement guarantee. We've priced this competitively against [Competitor A] and [Competitor B]. That said, if you're comfortable with a non-exclusive arrangement, I could move you down to [X-2]%."

*InTime Response Option B (Offensive - Volume Play):*
"If you commit to [X] placements per year or longer term (2+ years), we could move to [X-3]% and build longer-term scale. How many hires per year are we talking?"

*InTime Response Option C (Creative):*
"What if we structure it as: [Lower rate] for first 5 placements, then [Standard rate] after? This way you can validate our quality, then commit longer-term?"

**Common MSA Sticking Points & Solutions:**

| Issue | Client Request | InTime Counter |
|---|---|---|
| Guarantee Period | "180 days" | "We can do 90 days standard, 120 days max for multi-year commitment." |
| Exclusivity | "We want non-exclusive" | "Non-exclusive is 5% higher fee. Or we can do exclusive within [specific skill/geography]." |
| Non-Comp Clause | "2-year non-compete nationwide" | "We'll agree to 1-year, within [City/Region] for [specific roles only]." |
| Data Security | "SOC 2 certified, HIPAA compliance required" | "We have [Certification A], and can add [Certification B] for additional fee if needed." |
| Rate Increase | "No increases for 3 years" | "We need CPI or 2.5% annual increase to offset cost growth. Fixed 2% annual increase is our minimum." |

**Step 5.2: Vendor Registration**

Many companies require vendor registration before engagement:

**Typical Vendor Registration Requirements:**
1. W-9 form (tax identification)
2. Insurance certificate (general liability, typically $1M+)
3. Compliance questionnaire (banking info, government relations, security practices)
4. Background check (company & potentially key employees)
5. Compliance certification (OFCCP compliance, FCRA compliance, I-9 verification)

**Timeline:** Vendor registration typically takes 5-10 business days; plan ahead.

**Action Item List:**
- [ ] Gather W-9 information (InTime's tax ID, address)
- [ ] Obtain insurance certificate of liability (get from InTime's insurance broker)
- [ ] Complete client's vendor registration form (detailed; may take 1-2 hours)
- [ ] Request background check details (if required)
- [ ] Submit package to client's procurement
- [ ] Follow up in 5 business days if not approved
- [ ] Once approved, confirm with client contact

### PHASE 6: ACCOUNT ONBOARDING AND HANDOFF

**Step 6.1: Account Manager Transition**

Once MSA is signed:

**Handoff Package to Account Manager:**
- Executed MSA and all related documents
- Contact list (primary contact, hiring manager, finance/procurement)
- Prospect research notes (company background, hiring priorities, decision-making style)
- Deal history (what was promised, rate, guarantee terms, exclusivity)
- Initial requisition details (roles, timeline, rate range, start date)
- Vendor registration confirmation (completion status)

**Initial Account Manager Call** (within 2 business days of signature):
- Duration: 30-45 minutes
- Attendees: Account Manager, client primary contact, hiring manager (if applicable)
- Agenda:
  - Relationship introductions
  - Job intake form (detailed requirement capture)
  - Process walkthrough (sourcing timeline, submission format, communication cadence)
  - Success criteria definition
  - First job requisition priority and timeline

**Step 6.2: Update Pipeline and Close Deal**

- Move deal from "MSA Execution" to "Closed Won"
- Document actual deal size (ACV based on MSA fee + estimated annual placements)
- Log in CRM with all terms
- Schedule 30-60 day check-in (BDM + Account Manager)
- Flag 6-month renewal conversation trigger (for BDM to conduct expansion/upsell)

## 8. KEY DECISION FRAMEWORKS

**Should I Continue Pursuing This Lead? (Go/No-Go Decision Matrix)**

Ask these questions:
1. Do they meet ICP criteria? (Yes / No / Partial)
2. Is there a real, immediate need? (Yes / No / Future)
3. Is the prospect a decision-maker or can they get us to one? (Yes / No)
4. Is there budget (explicit or implicit)? (Yes / No / Likely)
5. Is the timeline clear and reasonable? (Yes / No / Unclear)
6. Are they open to using external staffing? (Yes / No / Unsure)

**Scoring:**
- 6 yes = Pursue aggressively (prospect)
- 4-5 yes = Pursue moderately (nurture)
- 3 or fewer yes = Move to long-term nurture or drop (low probability)

**When to Escalate to Founder/VP Sales:**

- Deal size: >$300K annual contract value
- Strategic importance: Founder relationship, market entry, or key reference
- Executive buyer: C-level (CTO, CFO, CEO) requires founder credibility
- Complex negotiation: Legal, compliance, or terms significantly outside standard
- Stalled deal: Dead for 2+ weeks despite BDM effort; needs executive touch

## 10. COMMON SCENARIOS AND DECISION TREES

**Scenario 1: Prospect Not Responding to Outreach**

```
After 3 touches (LinkedIn message, 2 emails) with no response:

Option A: Warm Transfer
- Can anyone in InTime network refer you? (Founder, existing client, partner)
- If yes: Get warm intro; restart outreach from referral angle

Option B: Alternative Contact
- Can you identify different stakeholder at company? (Different VP Eng, HR Director)
- If yes: Research new contact; adjust outreach angle; restart sequence

Option C: Phone Outreach
- Do you have phone number? Can you dial directly?
- If yes: Make phone call; quick 30-sec pitch

Option D: Move to Nurture
- If no response after 4+ touches over 4 weeks: Move to nurture list
- Action: Send monthly "staying in touch" email; flag for outreach in 6 months
```

**Scenario 2: They Want Lower Fees Than Standard**

```
Client says: "Your fee is 30%, but I can get competitor for 20%."

Option A: Defend at Full Price
"Our fee includes [specific service]. If you go with competitor for 20%, what
happens if the candidate doesn't work out? We guarantee replacement for 90 days.
Do they?" [If no = justify fee]

Option B: Volume Play
"If you commit to [X] placements per year or [X]-year term, I can move to 25%.
That lock-in allows us to build capacity." [Get multi-year deal]

Option C: Partial Concession
"I can do 28% if this is exclusive and you commit to us as your primary vendor."
[Trade fee for exclusivity/volume]

Option D: Walk Away
"I understand the price sensitivity. Our model is built around quality and
guarantee. If price is the key driver, we may not be right fit. Let's explore
this more - what else matters besides fee?" [Get back to value prop]
```

**Scenario 3: Deal Stalled in Negotiation (2+ weeks)**

```
MSA is in client legal, no response for 2+ weeks.

Day 1: Email client contact: "Just checking in - any questions from legal team?"

Day 5: If no response: Phone call to client contact: "Hey, wanted to see if
there are any blockers from legal we can address. Happy to jump on a call
with their team."

Day 10: If still stalled: Escalate to founder/VP Sales for executive call
to client stakeholder: "I know legal is reviewing - are there specific concerns
we can address to help move this forward?"

Day 15: Decision: Offer to move forward on temp terms pending final signature?
Or move deal to "stalled" status?

Key: Don't let deals languish >2 weeks. Escalate and actively unblock.
```
