# InTime Cross-Functional Workflows & Operations
**Version 1.0 | Last Updated: February 2026**

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document outlines end-to-end workflows, team collaboration patterns, firm-wide operations rhythm, and communication protocols for InTime. It defines how teams work together to fulfill client jobs, build the candidate supply, manage client relationships, and execute daily/weekly/monthly operations.

**Organization Structure (16-person Phase 1 team):**
- Leadership: CEO
- Finance & Ops: Finance Lead, Operations Coordinator
- Supply: Back Office Recruiter, Pool Builder/Bench Sales
- Demand: Business Development Manager (BDM), Account Managers (2x)
- Delivery: Delivery Manager
- Academy: Training Lead
- HR: HR Manager
- Support: 1-2 additional (recruiting, admin)

**Escalation Triggers:**

- No candidate match available: Escalate to BDM (may require pay increase, timeline extension, or acknowledge low probability)
- Candidate rejects offer: Quick turnaround on alternative candidate
- Client rejects candidates: BDM + Account Manager review fit (may need to adjust profile)
- Start date at risk: Account Manager + Ops coordinate contingency plan

  - Clear subject line
  - Organized bullets or paragraphs

- Purpose: Document client interactions, candidate status, deals, placements
- Entries: Each client, job, candidate, placement gets a record
- Updates: Logged as they happen (same-day)
- Accessibility: Available to whole team (within role permissions)
- Searchability: Future reference, trend analysis

**Meetings (Synchronous discussion):**
- Use: Strategic decisions, complex discussions, relationship building
- Frequency: Per schedule in Section 3 (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- Participation: Invite relevant people only (respect time)
- Agenda: Shared 24 hours before (if possible)
- Notes: Record key decisions, action items
- Follow-up: Email summary if needed
