*The complete guide to managing training operations and student success.*

## Part 1: Role Overview

### Your Mission

**Ensure every training batch is full, every student succeeds, and top performers graduate placement-ready.**

You own the training funnel end-to-end:
- Fill training batches (9 students across 3 sessions)
- Ensure quality training delivery
- Track student progress
- Identify top performers for placement

**Training is our talent factory:** The better we train, the stronger our bench.

### Key Responsibilities

| Area | What You Own |
|------|--------------|
| **Enrollment** | Fill all training slots |
| **Operations** | Manage training schedule and logistics |
| **Quality** | Ensure training standards are met |
| **Progress** | Track and report student performance |
| **Coordination** | Sync with Trainer and Screening |
| **Top Performers** | Identify and handoff placement-ready graduates |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Batch fill rate | 100% (9/9 students) | Per batch |
| Student retention | 90%+ complete training | Per batch |
| Training quality score | 4.5+/5 | Per batch |
| Top performer rate | 20%+ pass at 90% | Per batch |
| Time to placement | <2 weeks post-graduation | Per graduate |
| Training revenue | ₹13.5L/cycle | Per cycle |

### Compensation Structure

| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Base salary | ₹75,000-1,00,000/month |
| Enrollment bonus | ₹5,000 per enrolled student |
| Completion bonus | ₹2,000 per graduate |
| Top performer bonus | ₹10,000 per placement |
| Target total | ₹1,25,000-1,75,000/month |

## Part 2: Understanding Training Operations

### Training Model

**Capacity:**
- 3 concurrent training sessions
- 3 students per session
- 9 students total at any time
- Rolling intake (new batch every 2 weeks)

**Revenue per Cycle:**
```
Training Fee: ₹1,50,000 per student
Students per cycle: 9
Revenue: ₹13,50,000 (₹13.5L)

Cycle length: 8 weeks
Monthly revenue: ~₹6.75L
```

```
Lead → Inquiry → Demo → Enrolled → Training → Graduated → Placed
  │        │        │        │          │          │          │
  └────────┴────────┴────────┴──────────┴──────────┴──────────┘
            TRAINING LEAD OWNS ENROLLMENT → GRADUATION
```

## Part 4: Enrollment Management

### Lead Sources

| Source | Owner | Your Role |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Website inquiries | Marketing | Follow up same day |
| Referrals | Current students | Incentivize referrals |
| LinkedIn | Training Sales | Qualify and close |
| Screening pipeline | Recruiters | Offer training to unqualified candidates |
| Job boards | Training Sales | Post training opportunities |

### Lead Qualification

**FAME Framework:**
- **F**it: Do they have the background to succeed?
- **A**bility: Can they afford training fees?
- **M**otivation: Are they committed to completing?
- **E**xpectations: Do they understand the program?

### Enrollment Conversation

**Discovery:**
```
"Tell me about your background and what brought you here."
"What's your goal - are you looking to switch careers or upskill?"
"Are you able to commit [X hours/week] to training?"
"How are you planning to fund the training?"
```

**Presenting the Program:**
```
"Here's how our [Technology] program works:

Duration: 8 weeks
Schedule: [Times]
Investment: ₹1,50,000

What you get:
• Live instructor-led training
• Hands-on projects
• Mock interviews
• Job placement support

Our top 20% of graduates get hired directly by us on W2.
Others get placement assistance and certification.

What questions do you have?"
```

**Closing:**
```
"Based on what you've shared, I think you'd be a great fit.

The next batch starts [date]. To secure your spot:
• Pay ₹50,000 deposit today
• Balance due before Day 1

Can I send you the enrollment form?"
```

### Enrollment Checklist

```
□ Background qualified
□ Motivation confirmed
□ Payment ability verified
□ Schedule compatibility checked
□ Expectations set
□ Deposit collected
□ Welcome email sent
□ Added to training system
□ Trainer notified
```

## Part 6: Student Management

### Onboarding New Students

**Pre-Training:**
- Welcome email with materials
- System access setup
- Schedule confirmation
- Mentor assignment (if applicable)

**Day 1:**
- Introduction session
- Program overview
- Expectations setting
- Peer introductions
- First assignment

### Weekly Student Check-Ins

**Check-In Agenda (15 min):**
```
1. How's training going? (1-10)
2. What's going well?
3. What's challenging?
4. Are you keeping up with assignments?
5. Any questions about the material?
6. What support do you need?
```

### Handling Struggling Students

**Early Intervention:**
```
"[Name], I noticed you've missed [X] sessions and are behind
on assignments.

I want to help you succeed. What's going on?

[Listen]

Here's what I suggest:
• [Specific support action]
• [Schedule adjustment if needed]
• [Extra help resources]

Can we commit to getting back on track this week?"
```

**If No Improvement:**
- Document conversations
- Formal warning
- Improvement plan
- Consider withdrawal (with partial refund if applicable)

### Top Performer Identification

**Criteria for Top 20%:**
- 90%+ on assessments
- 100% attendance (or excused)
- All assignments complete
- Strong mock interview performance
- Professional attitude

**Top Performer Actions:**
1. Early notification to Screening team
2. Priority interview prep
3. Direct W2 consideration
4. Success story documentation

## Part 10: Common Scenarios

### Scenario: Student Wants Refund

**Response:**
```
"I understand training isn't going as you expected.

Before we discuss refunds, can you tell me what's not working?
[Listen]

[If solvable] Let me propose some adjustments:
• [Different session time]
• [Extra support]
• [Other solution]

[If not solvable] I understand. Per our policy:
• Before Week 2: Full refund minus ₹10K admin fee
• Weeks 2-4: 50% refund
• After Week 4: No refund

Let me process this for you."
```

### Scenario: Trainer Unavailable

**Response:**
1. Find backup trainer or reschedule
2. Notify all students immediately
3. Offer make-up session
4. Document and address root cause

### Scenario: Low Enrollment

**Response:**
```
1. Analyze: Why are leads not converting?
2. Increase lead generation activities
3. Offer incentives (early bird, referral bonus)
4. Review pricing and messaging
5. Expand outreach channels
```
