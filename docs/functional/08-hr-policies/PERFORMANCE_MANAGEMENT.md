# Performance Management

How we evaluate, develop, and reward performance at InTime.

---

## Overview

Performance management is not about judging you. It's about:
- Clarity on expectations
- Regular feedback
- Support for growth
- Fair recognition

---

## Performance Cycle

| Phase | When | What |
|-------|------|------|
| Goal Setting | Start of quarter | Set OKRs and targets |
| Ongoing | Throughout | Regular check-ins, feedback |
| Review | End of quarter | Formal assessment |
| Development | Continuous | Growth planning |

---

## Goal Setting

### OKR Framework

**Objective:** What you want to achieve (qualitative)
**Key Results:** How you'll measure success (quantitative)

**Example:**
```
Objective: Build a strong talent pipeline

Key Results:
1. Source 400+ candidates this quarter
2. Achieve 30% screen-to-qualified rate
3. Deliver 10+ placement-ready candidates monthly
```

### Goal Categories

| Category | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Role Metrics | 60% | Core job performance |
| Team Goals | 20% | Contribution to team success |
| Development | 10% | Learning and growth |
| Values | 10% | Living company values |

### Setting Good Goals

- **Specific:** Clear and unambiguous
- **Measurable:** Quantifiable
- **Achievable:** Challenging but realistic
- **Relevant:** Aligned with role and company
- **Time-bound:** Clear deadline

## Regular Check-Ins

### Weekly Check-In (15-30 min)

**Purpose:** Stay aligned, remove blockers

**Agenda:**
1. What did you accomplish this week?
2. What are you working on next week?
3. Any blockers or challenges?
4. Any support needed?

### Monthly 1:1 (30-60 min)

**Purpose:** Deeper discussion on progress and development

**Agenda:**
1. Progress on monthly goals
2. What's going well?
3. What's challenging?
4. Feedback (both directions)
5. Development focus
6. Any concerns?

### Quarterly Review (60 min)

**Purpose:** Formal assessment and planning

**Agenda:**
1. Review OKRs and results
2. Performance rating discussion
3. Strengths and areas for growth
4. Goals for next quarter
5. Development plan
6. Career discussion

## Performance Rating

### Rating Scale

| Rating | Description | Definition |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Significantly exceeds all expectations |
| 4 | Exceeds | Consistently exceeds expectations |
| 3 | Meets | Fully meets expectations |
| 2 | Developing | Partially meets, needs improvement |
| 1 | Below | Does not meet expectations |

### Rating Distribution

We don't force distribution, but typically:
- 10-15% rate Exceptional
- 20-25% rate Exceeds
- 50-60% rate Meets
- 10-15% rate Developing or Below

### Rating Criteria

**Role Performance (60%)**
- Achievement of goals and metrics
- Quality of work
- Reliability and consistency

**Team Contribution (20%)**
- Collaboration
- Helping others succeed
- Team morale impact

**Growth & Values (20%)**
- Learning and development
- Living company values
- Initiative and ownership

## Feedback

### Giving Feedback

**SBI Model:**
- **S**ituation: When and where
- **B**ehavior: What they did (observable)
- **I**mpact: Effect of the behavior

**Example:**
```
"In yesterday's client call (Situation), you interrupted
the client several times (Behavior). This made them seem
frustrated and the call ended early (Impact)."
```

### Receiving Feedback

1. Listen fully before responding
2. Ask clarifying questions
3. Don't be defensive
4. Thank them for the feedback
5. Reflect and act on it

### Feedback Frequency

| Type | When |
|------|------|
| Positive | Immediately when you see it |
| Constructive | Promptly, privately |
| Formal | During scheduled reviews |

## Performance Improvement

### When Needed

A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is used when:
- Performance is consistently below expectations
- Previous coaching hasn't worked
- There are specific, documented issues

### PIP Process

1. **Documentation:** Manager documents specific issues
2. **Meeting:** Discuss concerns with employee
3. **Plan:** Create written improvement plan
4. **Support:** Provide resources and check-ins
5. **Review:** Assess progress at end of period
6. **Outcome:** Return to good standing OR next steps

### PIP Structure

```
Duration: Typically 30-60 days

Contents:
1. Specific issues to address
2. Expected performance standards
3. Support provided
4. Check-in schedule
5. Success criteria
6. Consequences if not met
```

### After PIP

| Outcome | Next Steps |
|---------|------------|
| Meets expectations | Return to normal performance cycle |
| Significant progress | Extend PIP with new goals |
| No improvement | Employment may be terminated |

## Career Development

### Development Planning

Every employee should have a development plan:
1. Career goals (1-3 years)
2. Skills to develop
3. Experiences needed
4. Timeline
5. Support required

### Development Activities

| Activity | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Stretch assignments | Build new skills |
| Mentoring | Learn from others |
| Training/courses | Formal learning |
| Job shadowing | Understand other roles |
| Projects | Apply skills |

### Career Conversations

Have career discussions with your manager:
- Quarterly: Check-in on development
- Annually: Deep career planning

## Calibration

### What It Is

Managers meet to discuss ratings and ensure consistency across teams.

### Purpose

- Fair comparison across roles
- Consistent standards
- Identify high performers
- Identify development needs

### Process

1. Managers propose ratings
2. Group discussion
3. Adjustments if needed
4. Final ratings approved
5. Communicate to employees

## FAQs

**Q: How often are reviews?**
A: Quarterly formal reviews, with weekly/monthly check-ins.

**Q: Can I disagree with my rating?**
A: Yes. Discuss with your manager. You can add comments to your review.

**Q: Does rating affect salary?**
A: Yes. Higher ratings typically mean higher raises and bonuses.

**Q: What if I'm on PIP?**
A: Focus on improvement. Meet regularly with manager. Ask for help.

**Q: How do I get promoted?**
A: Consistent high performance + demonstrated next-level skills + business need.
