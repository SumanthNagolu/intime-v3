# Code of Conduct

Standards of behavior for all InTime team members.

---

## Our Commitment

At InTime, we are committed to creating a workplace that is:
- Professional and respectful
- Inclusive and welcoming
- Safe for everyone
- Ethical and honest

This code applies to all employees, contractors, and partners.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Respect

- Treat everyone with dignity
- Value diverse perspectives
- Listen before judging
- Disagree respectfully

### 2. Integrity

- Be honest in all dealings
- Keep commitments
- Admit mistakes
- Do the right thing, even when hard

### 3. Professionalism

- Represent InTime well
- Meet your obligations
- Communicate clearly
- Take responsibility

### 4. Excellence

- Do your best work
- Continuously improve
- Help others succeed
- Take pride in quality

---

## Workplace Behavior

### Expected

- Arrive on time and be prepared
- Communicate professionally
- Meet deadlines and commitments
- Collaborate effectively
- Support team members
- Follow company policies

### Not Acceptable

- Harassment of any kind
- Discrimination
- Bullying or intimidation
- Dishonesty or fraud
- Violence or threats
- Substance abuse at work
- Theft or misuse of property

---

## Anti-Harassment

### What is Harassment?

Unwelcome conduct based on:
- Race or ethnicity
- Gender or gender identity
- Sexual orientation
- Religion
- Age
- Disability
- National origin
- Any protected characteristic

### Examples of Harassment

- Offensive jokes or comments
- Slurs or stereotyping
- Intimidation or threats
- Unwanted physical contact
- Displaying offensive materials
- Excluding someone deliberately

### Sexual Harassment

Includes but not limited to:
- Unwelcome sexual advances
- Requests for sexual favors
- Sexual comments or jokes
- Sharing sexual images
- Unwanted touching

### Zero Tolerance

We have zero tolerance for harassment. Violations result in disciplinary action, up to termination.

---

## Discrimination

### Our Policy

We do not discriminate in:
- Hiring
- Compensation
- Promotions
- Assignments
- Terminations
- Any employment decision

### Protected Characteristics

Decisions are made based on merit, not:
- Race, color, ethnicity
- Gender, gender identity
- Sexual orientation
- Religion
- Age
- Disability
- Marital status
- National origin
- Veteran status

---

## Conflicts of Interest

### What is a Conflict?

When personal interests interfere with company interests.

### Examples

- Working for a competitor
- Hiring relatives without disclosure
- Accepting gifts from vendors
- Having financial interest in a client/vendor
- Using company resources for personal gain

### What to Do

- Disclose potential conflicts to your manager
- Avoid situations that create conflicts
- Ask if you're unsure
- Never prioritize personal gain over company

---

## Confidentiality

### What's Confidential

- Client information
- Candidate information
- Company financials
- Trade secrets
- Employee information
- Business strategies

### Your Obligations

- Don't share confidential info outside work
- Secure documents and devices
- Use approved channels for communication
- Report security concerns

### After Employment

Confidentiality obligations continue after you leave InTime.

---

## Data Protection

### Handling Data

- Access only data you need for your job
- Never share login credentials
- Report data breaches immediately
- Follow data handling procedures

### Personal Data

- Collect only what's needed
- Keep it accurate and updated
- Secure it properly
- Delete when no longer needed

---

## Social Media

### Personal Accounts

- Make clear opinions are your own
- Don't share confidential information
- Don't disparage InTime, colleagues, or clients
- Be mindful that you represent InTime

### Professional Accounts

- Follow brand guidelines
- Keep it professional
- Approved content only
- No controversial topics

## Reporting Concerns

### How to Report

1. **Talk to your manager** (first option)
2. **Talk to HR** (if manager involved)
3. **Email founders** (for serious concerns)
4. **Anonymous reporting** (if available)

### What Happens

1. Report received
2. Investigation conducted
3. Action taken if warranted
4. Reporter updated (within limits)

### No Retaliation

- Reporting in good faith is protected
- Retaliation is strictly prohibited
- Report retaliation if it occurs

---

## Consequences

### Violation Response

Depending on severity:
1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Suspension
4. Termination
5. Legal action (if applicable)

### Factors Considered

- Severity of violation
- Intent
- History of conduct
- Impact on others
- Honesty when confronted
