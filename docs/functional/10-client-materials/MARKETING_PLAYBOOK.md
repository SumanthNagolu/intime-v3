# InTime Marketing Playbook

## 1. Marketing Strategy Overview

### Target Market Definition

**Primary Market:** Enterprise IT staffing and talent acquisition decisions in the United States

**Target Personas:**
1. **IT Hiring Managers** at Fortune 500 / large enterprises
   - In-house IT teams facing skills gaps
   - Project-based staffing needs
   - Technology-specialized hiring

2. **Vendor Managers** at large system integrators and implementation partners
   - Managing sub-vendor networks
   - Staffing gaps on active projects
   - Compliance and reliability concerns

3. **Procurement & MSP Leadership** at enterprises and staffing organizations
   - VMS/MSP platform administrators
   - Vendor panel optimization
   - SLA/compliance focus

**Geographic Focus:** United States (100% of sales focus)

**Industry Focus:** Technology-intensive industries
- Insurance (primary due to Guidewire specialization)
- Financial Services
- Healthcare
- Telecommunications
- Utilities
- Government/Federal contracting (growing)

---

### Brand Positioning

**Core Brand Statement:**
"InTime is a technology-specialized staffing firm that delivers pre-trained, certified IT consultants faster and more reliably than traditional staffing agencies."

**Key Differentiators:**

1. **Academy-Trained Talent**
   - Proprietary training academy (Guidewire, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Cloud)
   - Certified consultants ready for work (vs. raw talent)
   - Quality assurance before placement
   - Reduced ramp-up time for clients

2. **Proprietary SaaS Platform**
   - Modern, transparent requirement/placement workflow
   - Real-time tracking and communication
   - Compliance and audit trails
   - Better than manual/legacy processes

3. **Technology Specialization (Niche Focus)**
   - Deep expertise in enterprise tech platforms
   - Not generalist "body shop"
   - Guidewire specialists (insurance industry expertise)
   - Cloud, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Integration expertise

4. **Speed & Reliability**
   - 48-72 hour placement target (vs. industry 2-4 weeks)
   - Dedicated account management
   - Proactive pipeline management
   - Compliance-first approach

5. **Global Delivery, Local Service**
   - Hyderabad, India delivery hub (cost advantage)
   - US-based business development team
   - Understanding of US business culture and requirements
   - Time zone overlap for daily communication

---

### Marketing Budget Allocation

**Target Budget as % of Revenue: 2-5%**

**Budget Framework (assuming $5M annual revenue):**
- Total marketing budget: $100K-250K/year
- Monthly average: $8K-20K

**Channel Allocation (% of budget):**
- **LinkedIn (Primary):** 30-35% ($24K-58K)
- **Email Marketing:** 15-20% ($12K-41K)
- **Website/SEO/Blog:** 15-20% ($12K-41K)
- **Events & Sponsorships:** 15-20% ($12K-41K)

**Budget Scaling:** As revenue grows, increase marketing investment proportionally.

### 3.2 Email Marketing

**Why Email?**
- Highest ROI of all channels (typical 4:1 ROI)
- Direct communication channel (not algorithm-dependent)
- Permission-based (only send to opted-in contacts)
- Compliance: CAN-SPAM, SOB opt-outs mandatory

**Cold Outreach Email Campaigns:**

**5-7 Touch Email Sequence:**

**Touch 1 (Day 0) - Value Intro**
Subject: "InTime | [Company] IT Staffing Solutions"
- Personalized greeting (use first name)
- Specific trigger (new project, acquisition, hiring need)
- Value proposition (1-2 sentences)
- CTA: Soft ("Just wanted to reach out" / "Would love to connect")
- Signature with social links

**Touch 2 (Day 3) - Problem Validation**
Subject: "Re: InTime | [Company] IT Staffing Solutions"
- Acknowledge lack of response (don't be annoying)
- Validate their pain point with specific example
- Social proof (clients, certifications, metrics)
- CTA: "Let's schedule 15 minutes" (link to calendar)

**Touch 3 (Day 7) - Case Study / Social Proof**
Subject: "How [Similar Company] Solved [Problem] with InTime"
- Relevant case study or success story
- Metrics (time saved, cost reduction, etc.)
- Link to blog post or case study document
- CTA: "Schedule a quick call"

**Touch 4 (Day 14) - Objection Handling**
Subject: "About That Staffing Vendor..."
- Address likely objection (quality, cost, speed, compliance)
- Data or evidence refuting objection
- Free resource (whitepaper, guide, checklist)
- CTA: "Let's discuss your specific situation"

**Touch 5 (Day 21) - Different Angle**
Subject: "Your team's perspective on staffing"
- Shift to different pain point or angle
- New content piece (industry report, webinar recording)
- Slightly more direct CTA
- Mention "last attempt" to be transparent

**Touch 6 (Day 30) - Final Push (Optional)**
Subject: "One more thing before we stop reaching out"
- Limited-time offer (free consultation, discount)
- Honest tone ("We'll stop bugging you if not interested")
- Clear CTA and deadline

**Touch 7 (Day 45) - Final Farewell**
Subject: "We'll stop here (but the door is open)"
- Polite final message
- Leave door open for future
- No hard sell
- Add to nurture list

**Best Practices:**
- Personalize subject line and body with company/person name
- Keep emails short (under 150 words)
- Mobile-responsive (most emails read on mobile)
- One clear CTA per email
- Mix outreach with valuable content
- Track opens and clicks (optimize based on data)
- A/B test subject lines

**Nurture Campaigns (Warm Leads):**

**Weekly Nurture Series (for leads not ready to buy):**
- Week 1: Industry insight email (no pitch)
- Week 2: Blog article or resource email
- Week 3: Case study email
- Week 4: Product/service feature email
- Week 5-8: Repeat cycle
- Include opt-out link on every email

**Trigger-Based Emails:**
- Welcome series (when prospect joins mailing list)
- Webinar follow-up (24 hours after webinar)
- Demo follow-up (same day after meeting)
- Case study follow-up (relevant to prospect's industry)
- Referral thank you (when referred)

**Tools:**
- Email platform: Mailchimp, ConvertKit, HubSpot, or similar
- Capabilities needed: Automation, segmentation, A/B testing, reporting
- Budget: $50-200/month depending on volume

### 3.4 Job Board Presence

**Why Job Boards?**
- Candidates search for jobs on boards (recruitment channel)
- High visibility among IT job seekers
- Niche boards (Guidewire community) reach specialist candidates
- Build candidate pipeline for future placements

**Primary Job Boards (Post 1-2x per week):**

1. **Dice.com** (Primary for IT)
   - Largest IT job board in US
   - Target audience: IT professionals actively job hunting
   - Posting cost: $500-1,000/year subscription
   - Post IT staffing and consulting roles

2. **Indeed.com**
   - Largest general job board (wide reach)
   - Cost: ~$30-50 per job posting
   - Used by broader job seekers
   - Good for scaling candidate pool

3. **LinkedIn Jobs**
   - Integrated with LinkedIn profile
   - Organic visibility to LinkedIn users
   - Paid amplification available ($100-300 per posting)
   - Reach industry-specific professionals

4. **Monster.com**
   - Legacy but still used (especially by experienced professionals)
   - Cost: ~$30-50 per job posting
   - Secondary channel but maintains presence

5. **CareerBuilder.com**
   - General job board
   - Cost: ~$20-40 per job posting
   - Secondary channel

**Niche Job Boards (Specialist recruitment):**
- **Guidewire Community Forum** (lowest cost, highest relevance)
- **Insurance-specific boards** (Idealist for nonprofit, LinkedIn for general)
- **Cloud-specific boards** (AWS Jobs, Azure Jobs)

**Job Posting Strategy:**
- Post job titles as: "[Technology] Consultant | [Location] | Available Now"
- Include: Required skills, technologies, experience level, typical client types
- Make it clear: Consulting role, temporary/contract nature, InTime placement
- Include: Competitive rate, benefits, growth opportunities
- CTA: Apply now or contact

**Candidate Pipeline Management:**
- Track applicants by technology and experience level
- Build database of interested candidates
- Re-reach out to previous applicants for new opportunities
- Keep passive candidates engaged (monthly newsletter/update)

**Budget:** $2,000-3,000/month for all job boards

### 3.6 Referral Programs

**Why Referral Programs?**
- Lowest cost acquisition channel (only pay for results)
- Builds loyalty among existing clients and vendors
- Word-of-mouth is most trusted marketing
- Self-sustaining growth (referrers recruit for you)

**Three Referral Program Tracks:**

**Track 1: Client Referral Program**
- **Offer:** 5-10% discount on first placement from referred client
- **Mechanism:** Referrer mentions InTime to peer, provides contact
- **Follow-up:** InTime reaches out to referred contact, mentions referrer
- **Reward:** Discount applied to referrer's next invoice
- **Target:** Current IT managers and vendor managers

**Track 2: Consultant Referral Program**
- **Offer:** $1,000-$2,000 cash bonus for successful referral
- **Qualification:** Referred consultant must complete 90 days of work
- **Mechanism:** Consultant refers friend/colleague to InTime Academy
- **Promotion:** Mention in onboarding, quarterly email to all placed consultants
- **Payout:** Direct deposit after 90 days
- **Target:** All current and former InTime consultants

**Track 3: Employee Referral Program (Internal)**
- **Offer:** $500-$1,000 bonus for successful employee hire
- **Qualification:** Referred employee must pass 90 days of employment
- **Mechanism:** Employee refers candidate through ATS
- **Promotion:** Monthly mentions at all-hands, team Slack channel
- **Payout:** Direct deposit after 90 days
- **Target:** All InTime employees

**Referral Program Management:**
- Clear program rules (written document)
- Easy tracking system (spreadsheet or custom tracking)
- Regular reminders to potential referrers (monthly)
- Fast payout (within 2 weeks of qualification)
- Recognition/celebration (when referral is made and closed)

**Budget:** $50K/year for referral bonuses (scales with growth)

- Placements, engagements, assignments

**What NOT to Use:**
- Bodies, headcount, FTEs (dehumanizing)
- Temp workers (informal, belittling)
- Resources (too impersonal)
- Headhunters (old-fashioned, negative connotation)

## 6. Marketing Metrics and KPIs

### Key Metrics to Track

**Website Metrics:**
- **Traffic:** Total monthly sessions (target: 5,000+/month)
- **Bounce Rate:** % of visitors who leave without action (target: <50%)
- **Time on Site:** Average session duration (target: >2 min)
- **Form Fills:** Contact form submissions (target: 20-30/month)
- **Goal Completions:** Defined actions (email signup, demo request, phone call)
- **Conversion Rate:** Visitors to leads (target: 2-5%)

**LinkedIn Metrics:**
- **Followers:** Company page (target: 2,000-5,000+ by end of year)
- **Engagement Rate:** Likes, comments, shares per post (target: 3-5%)
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** Clicks to landing page (target: 5-10%)
- **InMail Open Rate:** % of InMails opened (target: 30%+)
- **InMail Response Rate:** % of recipients who respond (target: 10%+)

**Email Metrics:**
- **Open Rate:** % of emails opened (target: 25-35%)
- **Click Rate:** % of clicks in email (target: 3-5%)
- **Reply Rate:** % of recipients who reply (target: 3-5%)
- **Unsubscribe Rate:** (target: <0.5%)
- **List Growth:** New email subscribers (target: 50-100/month)

**Content Metrics:**
- **Blog Traffic:** Monthly organic visitors to blog (target: 2,000+)
- **Average Blog Shares:** Social shares per post (target: 5-10)
- **Time on Page:** Average reader time per blog post (target: >2 min)
- **Internal Links Clicked:** % of readers who click to services
- **Blog-to-Lead Conversion:** Blogs read before lead form fill

**Paid Advertising Metrics (LinkedIn, Google, etc.):**
- **Cost Per Lead (CPL):** Total ad spend / leads generated (target: <$100)
- **Cost Per Click (CPC):** Ad spend / total clicks (target: <$2)
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** Clicks / impressions (target: 2-3%)
- **Conversion Rate:** Clicks to lead forms (target: 5-10%)
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS):** Revenue / ad spend (target: 4:1)

**Pipeline Metrics:**
- **Marketing-Qualified Leads (MQLs):** Leads generated by marketing (target: 50-100/month)
- **Sales-Qualified Leads (SQLs):** Leads ready for sales (target: 20-40/month)
- **Conversion Rate:** MQL to SQL (target: 30-50%)
- **Marketing Pipeline Value:** Total value of leads in pipeline
- **Marketing-Attributed Revenue:** Revenue from marketing-sourced leads

**Brand Metrics:**
- **Brand Awareness:** % of target market aware of InTime (survey-based)
- **Brand Preference:** % who prefer InTime vs. competitors
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Client/vendor satisfaction (target: 50+)
- **Social Media Share of Voice:** Mentions of InTime vs. competitors
