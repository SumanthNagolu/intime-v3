## MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

**THIS MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT** (this "**Agreement**"), effective as of [EFFECTIVE DATE] (the "**Effective Date**"), is entered into by and between **[CLIENT LEGAL ENTITY NAME]**, a [STATE/COUNTRY] corporation with principal offices at [CLIENT ADDRESS] ("**Client**"), and **[INTIME LEGAL ENTITY NAME]**, a [STATE/COUNTRY] corporation with principal offices at [INTIME ADDRESS] ("**InTime**").

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, InTime is in the business of providing IT staffing, recruitment, and staffing-related services; and

**WHEREAS**, Client desires to engage InTime to provide staffing services on the terms and conditions set forth herein; and

**WHEREAS**, the parties wish to establish the framework and conditions under which InTime shall provide services to Client.

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

**1.1 Defined Terms.** As used in this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

**"Affiliate"** means any person or entity that, directly or indirectly, is controlled by, controls, or is under common control with the party in question.

**"Authorized Representative"** means an individual designated by a party in writing to act on its behalf regarding this Agreement.

**"Confidential Information"** has the meaning set forth in Article 6.

**"Consultant"** means any individual engaged by InTime and assigned to perform services for Client under a Statement of Work, whether classified as a temporary employee, independent contractor, or otherwise.

**"Effective Date"** means the date first written above.

**"Engagement"** means the assignment of a Consultant to perform services for Client under a SOW.

**"Force Majeure Event"** has the meaning set forth in Section 9.5.

**"Initial Term"** means [NUMBER] year(s) from the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated pursuant to Article 8.

**"Intellectual Property"** means all inventions, patents, trademarks, service marks, trade names, copyrights, trade secrets, know-how, and other intellectual property rights, whether registered or unregistered.

**"Losses"** means all damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses, including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs, arising from or related to a claim.

**"Services"** means the IT staffing and consulting services provided by InTime to Client as detailed in Article 2 and as further defined in each SOW.

**"SOW"** or **"Statement of Work"** means a document signed by authorized representatives of both parties that specifies the scope of services, Consultant qualifications, location, duration, rates, and other engagement-specific terms.

**"Work Product"** means all deliverables, documents, code, analyses, reports, and other materials produced by Consultant(s) in performance of Services under an Engagement.

## ARTICLE 2: SERVICES

**2.1 Scope of Services.** InTime agrees to provide IT staffing and consulting services to Client, including but not limited to:

- Identification and recruitment of qualified IT consultants and staff
- Screening, interviewing, and reference checking of candidates
- Placement of Consultants at Client's facilities or remotely as agreed
- Ongoing management and support of placed Consultants
- Replacement of Consultants if required and agreed upon
- Administrative and payroll support for Consultants

The scope of services shall be further defined in individual Statements of Work.

**2.2 Statement of Work Process.** All Engagements shall be documented in a SOW that includes:

- Description of services and project scope
- Required Consultant qualifications, experience, and skills
- Engagement location (on-site, remote, hybrid)
- Engagement duration and proposed start/end dates
- Bill rate, payment terms, and invoice schedule
- Hours of work and any overtime expectations
- Client's key contacts and reporting structure
- Any client-specific requirements (security clearances, training, compliance)
- Any special conditions or contingencies

SOWs shall be signed by authorized representatives of both parties and shall be incorporated by reference into this Agreement.

**2.3 Consultant Qualifications and Representations.**

- **InTime Representations:** InTime represents and warrants that each Consultant provided shall have skills and experience substantially matching the job requirements outlined in the applicable SOW.
- **Screening and Verification:** InTime shall conduct reasonable background checks, reference checks, and skills assessments prior to placement.
- **Client Verification:** Client has the right to directly verify Consultant qualifications, including technical assessments, before accepting placement.
- **No Misrepresentation:** InTime shall not misrepresent Consultant qualifications, work history, certifications, or credentials.

**2.4 Right to Interview and Reject.**

- **Client Right:** Client has the right to interview and assess potential Consultants before placement.
- **Rejection:** If Client rejects a proposed Consultant, InTime shall provide an alternative candidate within [NUMBER] business days at no additional charge.
- **Multiple Rejections:** If Client rejects [NUMBER] consecutive candidates, parties shall discuss revised requirements or InTime may revert to standard placement fee terms.

**2.5 Replacement Obligations.**

- **Performance-Based Replacement:** If a placed Consultant fails to perform satisfactorily or does not meet agreed-upon qualifications, InTime shall provide a replacement Consultant at no additional charge, subject to the 90-day guarantee in Article 4.
- **Replacement Timing:** InTime shall provide a replacement within [NUMBER] business days of notification.
- **Limited Replacements:** InTime's obligation to provide replacements without additional charge is limited to [NUMBER] replacements per Engagement. Thereafter, Client shall pay a replacement fee of [AMOUNT] per replacement.

## ARTICLE 3: FINANCIAL TERMS

**3.1 Bill Rates and Rate Cards.**

- **Rates:** The bill rate(s) for each Engagement shall be specified in the SOW and shall be the sole compensation payable by Client to InTime for Services.
- **Rate Cards:** InTime shall provide a current rate card to Client upon request, reflecting available Consultant roles, experience levels, and associated rates.
- **Transparency:** Rates are all-inclusive and include InTime's margins, management fees, recruiting fees, and administrative overhead.

**3.2 Invoice and Payment Terms.**

- **Invoicing Schedule:** InTime shall invoice Client [weekly / bi-weekly / monthly] based on Consultant timesheets and hours worked.
- **Invoice Content:** Each invoice shall identify:
  - Consultant name and role
  - Engagement period (dates)
  - Hours worked (validated via timesheets)
  - Bill rate per hour or fixed fee
  - Total amount due
  - SOW reference
  - Wire transfer or payment instructions

- **Payment Terms:** Client shall pay invoices within [30 / 45 / 60] days of invoice date (Net [30/45/60]).
- **Late Payments:** If Client fails to pay within the specified timeframe:
  - InTime may impose a late payment fee of [1% / 1.5% / 2%] per month (or maximum allowed by law)
  - InTime may suspend Services (with [NUMBER] days written notice)
  - InTime may pursue legal remedies for collection costs and attorneys' fees

**3.3 Rate Adjustments.**

- **Annual Review:** Bill rates shall be subject to annual review and may be adjusted based on market conditions, experience, and consultant performance.
- **Rate Increase Notice:** InTime shall provide [60/90] days written notice of any rate increases.
- **Freeze Period:** Rates shall not be increased more frequently than [annually / semi-annually].
- **Negotiation:** If Client objects to proposed rates, parties shall negotiate in good faith. If unable to agree, either party may terminate the SOW with [30] days written notice.

**3.4 Expenses.**

- **Included:** The bill rate includes all typical staffing and management costs (recruiting, screening, payroll processing, benefits administration, insurance).
- **Out-of-Pocket Expenses:** Any out-of-pocket expenses (travel, relocation, special training, certifications) shall be reimbursable only with Client's prior written approval.
- **Expense Documentation:** Reimbursable expenses must be documented with receipts and submitted within [30] days of incurrence.

## ARTICLE 4: EMPLOYMENT AND COMPLIANCE

**4.1 Employer of Record.**

- **InTime as Employer:** InTime, not Client, is the sole employer of each Consultant for all legal purposes.
- **Control and Direction:** While Client directs the day-to-day work and provides technical supervision, InTime retains control over employment matters (hire, fire, discipline, compensation).
- **No Joint Employment:** The parties acknowledge and agree that this arrangement does not create a joint employment relationship.

**4.2 Workers' Compensation.**

- **InTime Responsibility:** InTime shall maintain workers' compensation insurance covering all Consultants in accordance with applicable state/federal law.
- **Claims:** All workers' compensation claims shall be handled by InTime's insurance carrier and InTime.
- **Client Cooperation:** Client shall cooperate with InTime and insurance carriers in claims investigations as reasonably requested.

**4.3 Tax Obligations.**

- **W2 Consultants:** For Consultants classified as InTime employees (W2):
  - InTime is responsible for payroll tax withholding and remittance
  - InTime shall provide W2 forms and comply with all applicable tax laws
  - Client shall not withhold taxes from Consultant payments

- **1099 Consultants:** For Consultants classified as independent contractors (1099):
  - Consultant is responsible for all self-employment taxes
  - InTime shall provide 1099 forms and comply with reporting requirements
  - Client's payments to InTime are for InTime's services, not direct payments to Consultant

- **C2C (Corp-to-Corp):** For Consultants operating as separate business entities:
  - Consultant's entity is responsible for all tax obligations
  - InTime invoices Client, then pays Consultant's entity the agreed-upon amount

**4.4 Immigration Compliance.**

- **E-Verify:** InTime shall verify work eligibility of all Consultants through E-Verify (or equivalent) before placement.
- **I-9 Documentation:** InTime shall complete Form I-9 for all Consultants and maintain records.
- **Right to Work:** Each Consultant represents that they have the legal right to work in the United States.
- **Client Cooperation:** Client shall cooperate with InTime's verification process and shall not interfere with immigration status.

**4.5 Background Checks.**

- **Standard Screening:** InTime shall conduct criminal background checks for all Consultants prior to placement.
- **Drug Screening:** InTime shall coordinate drug screening (urinalysis or equivalent) as standard practice.
- **Client Requirements:** If Client has additional screening requirements (credit checks, driving records, security clearances), Client shall specify in SOW and may bear additional costs.
- **Confidentiality:** Background check results shall be kept confidential per FCRA and privacy laws.
- **Consent:** Each Consultant provides written consent to background and drug screening prior to placement.

**4.6 Non-Solicitation.**

- **Prohibition:** Client agrees not to hire, recruit, or solicit any Consultant for permanent employment without InTime's consent during the Engagement and for [12] months after termination.
- **Conversion Fee:** If Client hires a Consultant in violation of this Section, Client shall pay a conversion fee as follows:
  - Months 1-3: 30% of annual salary offered to Consultant
  - Months 4-6: 25% of annual salary offered to Consultant
  - Months 7-9: 20% of annual salary offered to Consultant
  - Months 10-12: 15% of annual salary offered to Consultant
  - After 12 months: No conversion fee

- **Calculation:** Annual salary = base salary only (excluding benefits, bonus, equity)
- **Payment Timing:** Conversion fee shall be due within [30] days of hire date

## ARTICLE 5: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**5.1 Ownership of Work Product.**

- **Client Ownership:** All Work Product created by Consultant(s) in performance of Services shall be owned exclusively by Client.
- **Transfer:** InTime hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in and to Work Product to Client effective upon creation.
- **Moral Rights:** To the extent permitted by law, Consultant(s) waive moral rights or rights of attribution in Work Product.

**5.2 Pre-Existing IP.**

- **InTime IP:** Nothing in this Agreement shall transfer ownership of InTime's pre-existing Intellectual Property (recruiting tools, methodologies, templates, training materials, platforms).
- **Client IP:** Client retains all ownership of its pre-existing IP and any improvements made thereto.
- **License Grant:** InTime grants Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use Consultant's work product solely for Client's business purposes.

**5.3 Third-Party IP.**

- **Representations:** Each party represents that it shall not infringe third-party IP rights in performing Services.
- **Client Responsibility:** Client is responsible for ensuring that any Client-provided materials, specifications, or requirements do not infringe third-party rights.
- **InTime Responsibility:** InTime shall not include any third-party open-source or licensed software without Client's prior written approval.

## ARTICLE 6: CONFIDENTIALITY

**6.1 Definition of Confidential Information.**

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party to the other, including but not limited to:

- Trade secrets, business plans, financial information, customer lists, pricing data
- Technical information, code, algorithms, databases, designs, specifications
- Consultant skill profiles, performance data, rates, and placement terms
- Processes, methodologies, and proprietary systems
- Any information marked as "Confidential" or reasonably understood to be confidential

Confidential Information does NOT include:

- Information that is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement
- Information that was independently developed without use of the other party's Confidential Information
- Information rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligations

**6.2 Confidentiality Obligations.**

- **Non-Disclosure:** Each party shall not disclose the other party's Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent.
- **Limited Use:** Confidential Information shall be used solely for performing or evaluating services under this Agreement.
- **Reasonable Care:** Each party shall use reasonable care (at least the same level as for its own confidential information) to protect Confidential Information.
- **Authorized Disclosures:** Parties may disclose Confidential Information to employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations.

**6.3 Exceptions to Confidentiality.**

Notwithstanding Section 6.2, a party may disclose Confidential Information:

- As required by law, regulation, court order, or government authority (with prompt notice to allow the other party to seek protective measures)
- In confidence to legal counsel, accountants, and advisors on a need-to-know basis
- In aggregate form for reporting or analytical purposes (that does not identify the other party)

**6.4 Duration.**

This Article shall survive termination of this Agreement for [2 / 3 / 5] years.

## ARTICLE 7: INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

**7.1 Mutual Indemnification.**

Each party (the "**Indemnifying Party**") shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other party, its affiliates, and their respective officers, directors, employees, agents, and successors from any Losses arising from or related to:

- The Indemnifying Party's breach of this Agreement
- The Indemnifying Party's violation of applicable law or regulation
- The Indemnifying Party's negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud
- InTime's violation of labor laws or employment laws (for InTime indemnification)
- Client's misuse or infringement of InTime's IP rights (for Client indemnification)
- Infringement of third-party IP rights caused by the Indemnifying Party's services or materials

**7.2 Limitation of Liability.**

EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY, IP INFRINGEMENT, OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS:

- **Limitation Cap:** Neither party's total liability under this Agreement shall exceed the total amount paid by Client to InTime in the [12] months preceding the claim.
- **Excluded Damages:** IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOST DATA, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY THEREOF.

**7.3 Insurance Requirements.**

InTime shall maintain and provide certificates of insurance for:

- **General Liability:** [AMOUNT] per occurrence / [AMOUNT] aggregate
- **Errors & Omissions (E&O):** [AMOUNT] per claim / [AMOUNT] aggregate
- **Workers' Compensation:** Statutory limits per applicable state
- **Cyber Liability Insurance:** [AMOUNT] per claim / [AMOUNT] aggregate (if applicable)
- **Proof:** InTime shall provide current certificates of insurance upon request and [annually / upon renewal].

Client may require additional or increased coverage depending on engagement scope.

## ARTICLE 8: TERM AND TERMINATION

**8.1 Term.**

- **Initial Term:** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for the Initial Term of [NUMBER] year(s), unless earlier terminated.
- **Renewal:** This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive [NUMBER]-year terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [60/90] days prior to expiration.
- **Individual SOWs:** Individual SOWs may have different durations and shall expire per their terms, even if the MSA continues.

**8.2 Termination for Convenience.**

Either party may terminate this Agreement or any individual SOW for any reason or no reason upon [30 / 60 / 90] days written notice to the other party.

**8.3 Termination for Cause.**

Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if:

- The other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within [15 / 30] days of written notice
- The other party becomes insolvent, bankrupt, or ceases operations
- The other party is convicted of fraud, embezzlement, or a felony
- There is a Force Majeure Event lasting more than [30] days

**8.4 Effect of Termination.**

Upon termination or expiration:

- All SOWs shall terminate (unless stated otherwise)
- Consultant(s) shall cease work as of the effective termination date
- InTime shall invoice Client for all Services performed through the termination date
- Client shall pay all outstanding invoices within [15] days
- All confidentiality and IP ownership provisions shall survive
- Either party may use all non-confidential information gained

**8.5 Survival Provisions.**

The following provisions shall survive termination of this Agreement:

- Article 5 (Intellectual Property)
- Article 6 (Confidentiality)
- Article 7 (Indemnification and Liability)
- Section 3.1 (Payment Obligations)
- Article 9 (General Provisions - Governing Law, Dispute Resolution, Severability)
- Any other provision by its express terms

## ARTICLE 9: GENERAL PROVISIONS

**9.1 Governing Law.**

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [STATE / JURISDICTION], without regard to conflicts of law principles. Each party consents to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in [COUNTY/LOCATION].

**9.2 Dispute Resolution.**

- **Negotiation:** Any dispute arising from this Agreement shall first be addressed through good-faith negotiation between authorized representatives of the parties within [15] days of written notice.
- **Mediation (Optional):** If negotiation fails, either party may propose non-binding mediation before a neutral third-party mediator.
- **Litigation:** If not resolved through negotiation or mediation, either party may pursue litigation in the courts specified in Section 9.1.
- **Costs:** Each party shall bear its own costs of dispute resolution unless a court awards costs to the prevailing party.

**9.3 Force Majeure.**

- **Definition:** A "Force Majeure Event" means any event beyond the reasonable control of a party, including acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, government action, natural disasters, labor strikes, or utility failures.
- **Suspension:** If a Force Majeure Event prevents a party from performing its obligations for more than [30] days, that party may suspend performance without breach.
- **Termination Right:** If a Force Majeure Event prevents performance for more than [60] days, either party may terminate the affected SOW(s) without penalty.
- **Notice:** The affected party shall provide prompt written notice and describe the nature and expected duration of the impact.

**9.4 Assignment.**

- **Restriction:** Neither party may assign or delegate this Agreement or any SOW without the prior written consent of the other party (not to be unreasonably withheld).
- **Exception:** InTime may assign to an affiliate or successor entity with written notice to Client.
- **Binding Effect:** Any attempted assignment in violation of this Section is void.

**9.5 Notices.**

All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by:

- Personal delivery
- Overnight courier (FedEx, UPS)
- Certified mail, return receipt requested
- Email (with read receipt request)

**Delivery Addresses:**

**For Client:** [ADDRESS] / Email: [EMAIL]

**For InTime:** [ADDRESS] / Email: [EMAIL]

Notice is effective upon receipt.

**9.6 Amendment and Modification.**

This Agreement may only be amended or modified by written agreement signed by authorized representatives of both parties. No oral agreements, email agreements, or implied modifications shall be binding.

**9.7 Entire Agreement.**

This Agreement (including all SOWs and exhibits) constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, proposals, understandings, and agreements (oral and written). No representations or warranties are made except as expressly stated herein.

**9.8 Severability.**

If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction:

- That provision shall be severed from the Agreement
- The remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect
- The parties shall negotiate in good faith to replace the invalid provision with a valid provision achieving the original intent

**9.9 Counterparts and Execution.**

This Agreement may be executed in multiple counterparts (including digital, PDF, or email), each of which shall be deemed an original, and together which shall constitute one instrument. Signature pages may be executed separately and exchanged electronically.

**9.10 Waiver.**

No waiver of any provision or breach of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving party. A waiver of one breach does not constitute a waiver of any other breach.

**9.11 Independent Contractors.**

InTime Consultants are independent contractors or employees of InTime, not employees of Client. This Agreement does not create a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

**9.12 Relationship of Parties.**

InTime shall maintain complete control over the recruitment, hiring, management, termination, and compensation of Consultants. Client shall not supervise, train (beyond project-specific direction), or make employment decisions regarding Consultants.

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR LEGAL COUNSEL

**Key Customization Points:**

1. **Governing Law:** Customize to match Client's preferred jurisdiction and match your incorporation state if beneficial.
2. **Payment Terms:** Adjust Net 30/45/60 based on client creditworthiness and cash flow needs.
3. **Non-Solicitation:** Conversion fee structure and timeline should reflect true placement costs and risk.
4. **Liability Cap:** Adjust limitation of liability caps based on your risk tolerance and typical engagement values.
5. **Insurance Requirements:** Update coverage amounts based on client size, industry (healthcare, finance, defense), and engagement scope.
6. **Tax Classification:** Verify appropriate classification of Consultants (W2 vs. 1099) to avoid IRS reclassification risk.
7. **Indemnification:** Consider adding specific indemnity for IP infringement by Consultants; may require back-to-back indemnity from Consultants.

**Risk Mitigation Considerations:**

- Add specific language regarding Client's obligation to provide safe working environment
- Consider adding provisions for background check disputes or discrepancies
- Add audit rights allowing Client to verify hours/performance
- Add provisions requiring Consultants to use Client-approved tools/methodologies to minimize liability
- Consider adding specific performance standards and Client remedies for non-performance
